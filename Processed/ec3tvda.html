go n.2

[abbr.]

(Aus.) a goanna.

1900Bulletin (Sydney) 7 July 14/2: I found a large black-and-yellow gent with all that remained (about a foot of tail) of a brother \xe2\x80\x98go.\xe2\x80\x99 sticking out of his mouth.
1901Bulletin (Sydney) 13 July 15/1: When James rushed, the go. slid up a tree with the strings of the leather bag hanging from its mouth.
1912Dubbo Dispatch (NSW) 29 Mar. 7/8: When last seen the old chap had an enormous black and white \xe2\x80\x98go\xe2\x80\x99 tied up with a chain.
1955N. Pulliam I Travelled a Lonely Land (1957) 186: Since the real iguana doesn\xe2\x80\x99t live in Australia, \xe2\x80\x98goanna\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 sometimes shortened to \xe2\x80\x98go\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 has become firmly accepted.
1969L. Hadow Full Cycle 139: If there\xe2\x80\x99s any blue this time, I\xe2\x80\x99m heading north like a go up a tree. They can stick this joint.
