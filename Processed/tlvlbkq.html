head v.1

[the betting on \xe2\x80\x98heads or tails\xe2\x80\x99]
(Aus.)

1. (also headings) usu. as head \xe2\x80\x99em: to play \xe2\x80\x98two-up\xe2\x80\x99 [cite 1902 suggests any form of gambling].

1886Eve. News (Sydney) 22 May 3/6: We went down into the hollow, and we had a very fair game. Some of us \xe2\x80\x98headed \xe2\x80\x98em.\xe2\x80\x99 some went at pitch-and-toss, and two lots went for \xe2\x80\x98nap\xe2\x80\x99 [...] Anyhow, we enjoys our Sunday, and I can\xe2\x80\x99t see no more harm in playing headings [...] on Sunday.
1889Dead Bird (Sydney) 9 Nov. 6/2: No sooner are the crowd set down [...] than a \xe2\x80\x98two up\xe2\x80\x99 or \xe2\x80\x98heading \xe2\x80\x99em\xe2\x80\x99 school starts.
1891Dead Bird (Sydney) 17 Jan. 1/2: Taught Juno to \xe2\x80\x98head \xe2\x80\x99em\xe2\x80\x99 and Doto to dance.
1902\xe2\x80\x98Dads Wayback\xe2\x80\x99 in Sun. Times (Sydney) 16 Nov. 5/5: \xe2\x80\x98[T]hey jines er gaffin\xe2\x80\x99 school an\xe2\x80\x99 heads \xe2\x80\x99em, or plays two up an\xe2\x80\x99 ther like o\xe2\x80\x99 that\xe2\x80\x99.
1905Argyle Liberal (NSW) 7 Nov. 2/4: I approached him while Bob went in the direction of Ready, who was engaged \xe2\x80\x99eaden \xe2\x80\x99em with one of the young R\xe2\x80\x94\xe2\x80\x99s.
1915C.J. Dennis \xe2\x80\x98A Spring Song\xe2\x80\x99 in Songs of a Sentimental Bloke 15: Jist \xe2\x80\x99eadin\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x99em, an\xe2\x80\x99 doin\xe2\x80\x99 in me gilt.
1920Truth (Brisbane) 11 Jan. 11/6: [headline] IS TWO-UP TWO-UP WHEN IT\xe2\x80\x99S CALLED HEADING \xe2\x80\x99EM?

2. (Aus.) to toss up for.

1889Dead Bird (Sydney) 24 Aug. 7/2: He headed the boys for the bundle and came out half a caser a head.

3. to throw \xe2\x80\x98heads\xe2\x80\x99 in a game of two-up.

1949L. Glassop Lucky Palmer 100: And he\xe2\x80\x99s \xe2\x80\x94 headed \xe2\x80\x99em!
1966J. Holledge Great Aust. Gamble 100: The spinner bets that he will \xe2\x80\x98head \xe2\x80\x99em\xe2\x80\x99, and naturally the others are backing tails [ibid.] 101: They hit the ground, and from every throat (if they are the same) comes the cry: \xe2\x80\x98He\xe2\x80\x99s headed \xe2\x80\x98em,\xe2\x80\x99 or \xe2\x80\x98He\xe2\x80\x99s tailed \xe2\x80\x99em\xe2\x80\x99.
