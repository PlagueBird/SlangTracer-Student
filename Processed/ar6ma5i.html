deuce v.

[deuce n.1 (6)/deuce n.1 (9a)]

1. (Aus.) to shear approximately 200 sheep in one day.

1950J. Sorenson Coll. Poems 32: I thought perhaps he may have chanced to hear / Of how I almost deuced them* at Murgoo. *\xe2\x80\x99Deuce them\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x93 To shear 200 sheep [GAW4].

2. (US drugs) to sell two marijuana cigarettes.

1940 \xe2\x80\x98Jargon of Marihuana Addicts\xe2\x80\x99 in AS XV:3 Oct. 337/1: to deuce someone. To sell someone two cigarettes.

\n In exclamations\n

deuce it up!

(US prison) a command for the prisoners to form two lines.

2023Rayman & Blau Riker\xe2\x80\x99s 233: [U]sually we would yell, \xe2\x80\x98Quiet down and deuce it up!\xe2\x80\x99 But this time, I said, \xe2\x80\x98Gentlemen, please give me two lines\xe2\x80\x99.
