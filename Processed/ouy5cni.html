chip n.3

[chippie n.1 (1)]
(US)

1. a promiscuous woman, esp. a prostitute.

1892W. Norr Stories of Chinatown 29: Why, you can\xe2\x80\x99t walk a step without brushing up against some \xe2\x80\x98chips\xe2\x80\x99.
1924(con. 1880\xe2\x80\x931924) F.J. Wilstach Anecdota erótica 32: Some men have chips on their shoulders; others prefer them on their laps.
1933N. Algren \xe2\x80\x98So Help Me\xe2\x80\x99 in Texas Stories (1995) 16: That Jew didn\xe2\x80\x99t know what that chip wanted a-tall.
1942 in G. Legman Limerick (1953) 204: There was a young chip from Brazil / Who fucked like a veritable mill.

2. a woman.

1894Mirror of Life : [US speaker] \xe2\x80\x98What th\xe2\x80\x99; another scrap over me, hey? Well, fight it out. Th\xe2\x80\x99 chip that wins gets me. See?\xe2\x80\x99.
1895E. Townsend Chimmie Fadden Explains 122: De Duchess and dat chip Maggie! say, dey\xe2\x80\x99d put your eyes out in a minute if ever you\xe2\x80\x99d see em unexpected.
