whack n.2

also wack
[whack v.1 ]

1. a blow, usu. with some form of stick; thus constr. with the, physical violence see cite 1914).

1793J. O\xe2\x80\x99Keeffe Sprigs of Laurel 14: He gave her a smack. / He gave me a whack.
1811J. Poole Hamlet Travestie II iii: He\xe2\x80\x99ll soon be here: \xe2\x80\x94 then let him have his whack: Tell him he\xe2\x80\x99ll bring a house upon his back.
1824Pierce Egan\xe2\x80\x99s Life in London 19 Sept. 269/1: [W]hat a beautiful whack between the peepers he had given his friend Phelim O\xe2\x80\x99Shaughnesy at a little bit of a blow up on the road.
1832Egan Bk of Sports 263: [note] Now this brought on a general fight, Lord, what a gallows row \xe2\x80\x94 / With whacks and thumps throughout the night.
1842Whip & Satirist of NY & Brooklyn (NY) 2 July n.p.: [F]eel the whacks of our editorial cudgel .
1845J. Lindridge Sixteen-String Jack 139: I pardons the whack on account of the wisdom that moved the whacker.
1859Hotten Dict. of Modern Sl. etc. 115: Whack, or whacking a blow, or a thrashing.
1867\xe2\x80\x98Artemus Ward\xe2\x80\x99 Artemus Ward in London in Complete Works (1922) 439: \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99ll fondle him with a club,\xe2\x80\x99 I anserd, hitting him another whack.
1873W.H. Thomes Bushrangers 401: I\xe2\x80\x99d give yer a whack in the face if yer offered to take any advantage of me out here.

1900Punch 28 Feb. 157/3: To deal another whack on the head to a floundering Opposition.
1910F.P. Dunne Mr Dooley Says 176: WE give him th\xe2\x80\x99 same threaty rights he\xe2\x80\x99d give us, a dhrink an\xe2\x80\x99 a whack on th\xe2\x80\x99 head.

1922\xe2\x80\x98Sapper\xe2\x80\x99 Black Gang 359: I got a whack on the back of the head from something.

1938W. Winchell \xe2\x80\x98On Broadway\xe2\x80\x99 12 Mar. [synd. col.] A. Woollcott spent lots of time getting under the skin of actors, but once in a while he took a whack at himself.

1962R. Cook Crust on its Uppers 58: She\xe2\x80\x99d get bird in [...] to take the whacks for a tenner.
1970E. Tidyman Shaft 72: He considered giving the door a solid whack.
1982Beano 3 Apr. 20: Give him a hard whack with my feather duster.
1990R.G. Barratt \xe2\x80\x98Who\xe2\x80\x99s Jack of Robbo?\xe2\x80\x99 in What Do You Reckon (1997) [ebook] He\xe2\x80\x99s never been drunk, never copped a whack on the chin, never had his dabs taken.

2022J. Hannaham Didn\xe2\x80\x99t Nobody Give a Shit 94: Which gang of convicts or COs would take the next whack? Would these particular scumbuckets finish her off?

2. a second-rate or inexperienced coachman [his reliance on the whip].

1894Morn. Call (S.F.) 23 May 11/1: Slang from London Streets [...] An inexperienced Jehu may be either a \xe2\x80\x98gardener\xe2\x80\x99 or a \xe2\x80\x98whack\xe2\x80\x99.

3. (US Und.) a prison sentence.

1940W.R. Burnett High Sierra in Four Novels (1984) 297: You could get used to anything, even being without women and doing a long whack in solitary.
1971J. McNeill Chocolate Frog (1973) 23: Shirker: Yer got yer mate two years for his whack...\xe2\x80\x99n six months for yerself!
2006B. Matthews Intractable [ebook] They were going to take him back to Victoria when he finished his whack up here.

4. (Scot./Aus.) a punishment, one\xe2\x80\x99s deserts.

1993Smith & Noble Neddy (1998) 117: My half-brother Edwin, who had pleaded guilty to heroin charges, was sentenced to 10 years for his trouble. Right whack for him.
1993M.B. \xe2\x80\x98Chopper\xe2\x80\x99 Read How to Shoot Friends 95: Just ask Kevin Taylor. Oh, I forgot, you can\xe2\x80\x99t. He got his right whack in Pentridge.

5. (drugs) the act of diluting bulk drugs, e.g. heroin, for retail sale.

1989Pileggi & Scorsese Goodfellas [film script] 105: I had to get to Robin\xe2\x80\x99s house to give the package a whack with some quinine.

6. skill, ability.

2007D.S. Mitchell Killer Tune (2008) 24: Now everyone wanted a slice of his lyrical whack.

7. (Scot.) speed.

2022A. Parks May God Forgive 42: \xe2\x80\x98Bloody lorry must have been going at some whack\xe2\x80\x99.
