jack n.3

[SE jack, a device for lifting things]

1. (also jacko, jacks) the penis.

1604Dekker Honest Whore Pt 1 I i: [He] taught her to play upon the Virginals, and still his Jacks leapt up.
1834 \xe2\x80\x98Parody on Love\xe2\x80\x99s Ritornella\xe2\x80\x99 Flash Chaunter 44: Your Jack you have slip\xe2\x80\x99d / (Of time) in the nick.
1840\xe2\x80\x98The Female Gamester\xe2\x80\x99 in Facetious Songster in Spedding & Watt (eds) Bawdy Songbooks (2011) III 271: Whene\xe2\x80\x99er she deals she cribs or steals, / And can always lug out Jack.
1875E. Sellon New Epicurean 21: Away flew too material buttons, and Jack sprang out of his box into her hand.
1882Cremorne I 28: This fresh bit of fish made Jacko rise again.
1898\xe2\x80\x98Mary Suckit\xe2\x80\x99 Yvonne 24: Employing terms like «prick, Jack, rammer», the gentleman [...] took out his bursting cock.
1927Immortalia 9: Pete war thar with every tack, / And kept a-lettin\xe2\x80\x99 out more jack.
1945 in Randolph & Legman Ozark Folksongs and Folklore (1992) II 670: [as cit. 1927].

2. an erection; thus on jack adj., erect; thus often as sexual desire.

1890\xe2\x80\x931904Farmer & Henley Sl. and Its Analogues.
1959 personal recollection: on jack.
1963A. Baron Lowlife (2001) 26: From too much staying in bed I got the jack up to my eyebrows.
1988(con. 1940s) C. Bram Hold Tight (1990) 185: I [...] \xe2\x80\x99jaculate my jack / Into some seafood mama.
1989J. Morton Lowspeak 78: Jack \xe2\x80\x93 erection, as in \xe2\x80\x98I had a jack up to my eyebrows\xe2\x80\x99.

3. copulation.

1984Partridge DSUE (8th edn) 607: since ca. 1950.

4. an act of masturbation.

1984C. White Life and Times of Little Richard 128: I haven\xe2\x80\x99t had my morning jack yet!
2008Simon & Burns \xe2\x80\x98Bomb in the Garden\xe2\x80\x99 Generation Kill ep. 7 [TV script] Takes me to this porn shop, so he can have a jack in the booths.

5. (US black) a gun.

1995L. Stavsky et al. A2Z.
