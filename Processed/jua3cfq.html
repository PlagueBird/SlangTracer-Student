pitch v.

[fig. use of SE pitch, to throw]

1834News (London) 15 Nov. 383: Vy, I von\xe2\x80\x99t pitch no gammon, but this here\xe2\x80\x99s the pint.

3. (UK Und.) to pass counterfeit coins.

1858A. Mayhew Paved with Gold 363: There is one who swags \xe2\x80\x94 that is, carries the coin; there is another who pitches or passes it.
