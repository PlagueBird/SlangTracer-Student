rig v.1

also rig out, rig up
[naut. imagery]

1. to clothe; thus rigged out or rigged up, dressed (up).

1605Chapman & Jonson Eastward Ho! I i: If a privy search should be made, what sort of furniture are you rigged now?
1626Jonson Staple of News II i: She is not rigged, sir; setting forth some lady Will cost as much as furnishing a fleet.
1640The Wandering Jew 35: Some Gally-asse richly rigg\xe2\x80\x99d, and I warrant richly laden.
1676Wycherley Plain-Dealer IV i: You shall see how I rigg\xe2\x80\x99d my Squire out, with the remains of my shipwracked Wardrobe.
1686\xe2\x80\x9388 \xe2\x80\x98The Invincible Pride of Women\xe2\x80\x99 in Ebsworth Roxburghe Ballads (1893) VII:1 21: Is it her forecast to contrive to rise about the hour of Noon, / And if she\xe2\x80\x99s trimm\xe2\x80\x99d and rigg\xe2\x80\x99d by five, why this I count is very soon.
1696J. Dunton Night-Walker Sept. 16: I met with a Gentlewoman very finely rigg\xe2\x80\x99d.
c.1700London-Bawd (1705) 67: [He] soon perceived how he had been impos\xe2\x80\x99d upon; and furnish\xe2\x80\x99d him with more money to new Rig himself.
1713W. King York Spy 32: After we were rigg\xe2\x80\x99d, we took a Walk into the Town.
1723C. Walker Authentick Memoirs of Sally Salisbury 17: The Hospitable Dame [...] soon rigg\xe2\x80\x99d her out fit to adorn the Side-Box at an Opera.
1732Proceedings Old Bailey 5 July 169/2: I ask\xe2\x80\x99d him how he came to be so well Rigg\xe2\x80\x99d?
1744Newcastle Courant 23 June 2/3: We all contributed to new rig her. My Morning Gown made her a Gown and Petticoat.
1748\xe2\x80\x9349Cleland Memoirs of a Woman of Pleasure (1985) 13: In were brought two bundles of linen and wearing apparel; in short, all the necessaries for rigging me out, as they termed it, compleatly.
1757Foote Author in Works (1799) I 136: He\xe2\x80\x99s very young, and exceedingly well rigg\xe2\x80\x99d.
1775Gentleman\xe2\x80\x99s Mthly Intelligencer Aug. 409/1: Every doxy that had been constant in the harbour, was now new rigged at the expence of her lover.
c.1780Correct List of the Sporting Ladies [broadsheet] She is extremely well rigged from head to Stern, being lately in keeping by Ned Neptune, a jolly Tar.
1789G. Parker Life\xe2\x80\x99s Painter 62: We shortly after rigged her with an entire new and very neat change of wearables.
1790\xe2\x80\x98Bumper Allnight. Esquire\xe2\x80\x99 Honest Fellow 93: By G\xe2\x80\x94 thou art a tight little frigate, and well rigg\xe2\x80\x99d too.
1808B. Bradshaw Hist. of Billy Bradshaw 16: I had new-rigged her also from the heel upwards.
1818Byron Beppo v: You like by way of doublet, cape, or cloak, Such as in Monmouth Street, or in Rag Fair, would rig you out in seriousness or joke.
1821\xe2\x80\x935\xe2\x80\x98Bill Truck\xe2\x80\x99 Man o\xe2\x80\x99 War\xe2\x80\x99s Man (1843) 212: Rigged out in full holiday canonicals.
1833 \xe2\x80\x98The Chummies\xe2\x80\x99 Society\xe2\x80\x99 Fun Alive O! 54: I rigged my two \xe2\x80\x99prentices out.
1836D. Crockett in Meine Crockett Almanacks (1955) 54: Betsey herself come out all rigged up in her best bib and tucker.
1841 \xe2\x80\x98Go For Go: or a Bit On The Sly\xe2\x80\x99 Gentleman\xe2\x80\x99s Spicey Songster 15: He rigged himself out from top to toe.
1848\xe2\x80\x98Ned Buntline\xe2\x80\x99 Mysteries and Miseries of N.Y. III 59: So good a one that you\xe2\x80\x99d never know him if you met him, rigged up as he now is!

1855Broadway Belle (NY) 26 Feb. n.p.: Josh returned home a little earlier [...] to \xe2\x80\x98rig up,\xe2\x80\x99 as he termed it.

1856Abbeville Banner (SC) 19 June 1/5: His mother [...] rigged out in a beautiful jacket.
1861Hills & Plains I 115: Full-rigged Mrs. Ochter, with the yacht Esther gliding \nalong in her wake, sailed graciousily in.

1875\xe2\x80\x98Aliph Cheem\xe2\x80\x99 Lays of Ind (1905) 97: It was Cornet O'Leary rigged / Up as his counterpart!

1885M. Davitt Leaves from a Prison Diary I 27: She [i.e. the female thief] must, above all things, be well dressed. Thus \xe2\x80\x98rigged,\xe2\x80\x99 she makes her descent upon a fashionable jeweller, or dealer in other costly articles of female luxury.
1887G. Devol Forty Years a Gambler 199: Old Bill came up, rigged out just as I had seen him so many times before.
1897E.W. Townsend Chimmie Fadden 35: Well, I had me buttons off, and was rigged up in me new dude harness.
1899C. Rook Hooligan Nights 135: Rigged meself out like the Duke of Barnet Fair.
1900Bulletin (Sydney) 27 Jan. 13/2: There will now be a hatter and a shoemaker [...] in the Labor corner, and if tailor John M\xe2\x80\x99Mahon at last gets in for Fitzroy, the corner will be able to rig-out the whole House, if it only gets the job.
1900\xe2\x80\x98Doss Chiderdoss\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98Out for the Day and In for the Night\xe2\x80\x99 Sporting Times 9 June 1/4: She was rigged up regardless of cost, as if cash / Was no object, when used in such sort / As to yield a result calculated to smash / All the donahs who lived up her court.
1902Sun (NY) 12 Oct. 18/2: Dippy\xe2\x80\x99s wife rigged herself out in about a million dollars\xe2\x80\x99 worth of the happy fall rags .
1908\xe2\x80\x98O. Henry\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98The Ethics of Pig\xe2\x80\x99 Gentle Grafter (1915) 227: He was rigged up in the ready-made rutabaga regalia.
1914C. Holme Lonely Plough (1931) 189: Oh, Lanty will rig you out!

1970A. Sillitoe Start in Life (1979) 87: It wasn\xe2\x80\x99t the sort of job you could ever boast about in Borstal, but at least it kept me alive, and rigged my brothers and sister in good clothes.

2. in fig. use, to \xe2\x80\x98patch up\xe2\x80\x99.

1915C.E.W. Bean Anzac Book 132: We keep their clothing up to dick, / Equip and arm \xe2\x80\x99em, too; / We rig out the returning sick / Almost as good as new.
