tip n.1

[SE tip a glass or abbr. tipple n. (1)]

a draught of liquor; thus drink in general.

c.1698B.E. Dict. Canting Crew n.p.: Don\xe2\x80\x99t spoil his Tip, don\xe2\x80\x99t baulk his Draught. A Tub of good Tip, (for Tipple) a Cask of strong Drink.
1725New Canting Dict. [as cit. c.1698].
1737, 1759, 1760, 1776Bailey Universal Etym. Eng. Dict. [as cit. c.1698].
1738Swift Polite Conversation 64: miss.: (With a Glass in her Hand.) Hold your Tongue, Mr. Neverout, don\xe2\x80\x99t speak in my Tip.
1785, 1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1823Egan Grose\xe2\x80\x99s Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.
1832Spirit of the Times (NY) 31 Mar. 2/3: [A]fter another \xe2\x80\x98tip at the canteen,\xe2\x80\x99 [we] started for the woods.

tip-merry \n (adj.)\n

see separate entry.

\n In phrases\n

have a tip on \n (v.)\n

to be drunk.
