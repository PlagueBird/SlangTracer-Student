blue n.1

1. a \xe2\x80\x98blue stocking\xe2\x80\x99 [abbr. SE blue-stocking, a term that originated c.1750 when a coterie of intellectual ladies \xe2\x80\x93 Mrs Montague, Mrs Vesey and Mrs Ord \xe2\x80\x93 set out to replace London society\xe2\x80\x99s trad. post-dinner pursuits \xe2\x80\x93 card-playing \xe2\x80\x93 with more cerebral amusements. Formal dress was no longer required and among those who attended their soirées was Benjamin Stillingfleet, who habitually wore grey or blue worsted, instead of black silk, stockings. Admiral Boscawen, a staunch traditionalist, labelled these events \xe2\x80\x98the Blue Stocking Society\xe2\x80\x99; the ladies were called Blue Stockingers, then Blue Stocking Ladies and finally Blue Stockings].

1914Bulletin (Sydney) 31 Dec. 14/1: In fact, no kind of liquor on this planet has such a wide range of slang equivalents [as absinthe]. The Parisian absinthe drinker usually talks of \xe2\x80\x98taking a blue,\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98killing a worm,\xe2\x80\x99 or \xe2\x80\x98strangling a parrot.\xe2\x80\x99.
