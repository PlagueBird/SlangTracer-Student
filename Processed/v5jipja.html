whack n.1

also wack
[ext. uses of whack v.1 (1)]

1. (also whack in) a share, a portion.

1781G. Parker View of Society II 83: Your third rate class of sharpers, when they have won a sum of money, if they should happen to refuse a brother sharper who is flash to the rig, and has been a by-stander, his whack, are instantly snitched upon; that is, the Snitcher follows the loser, and asks him what he will give him (the Snitcher) if he puts him in the way of recovering his money?
1791H. Lemoine \xe2\x80\x98Education\xe2\x80\x99 in Attic Misc. 117: [of a group of condemned men] The dolman sounding, while the sheriff's nod, / Prepare the snitcher to dead hook the whack.
1809G. Andrewes Dict. Sl. and Cant.
1818\xe2\x80\x98A. Burton\xe2\x80\x99 Adventures of Johnny Newcome IV 203: She\xe2\x80\x99ll have a pretty whack of money!
1828\xe2\x80\x98A Flat Enlightened\xe2\x80\x99 Life in the West I 136: \xe2\x80\x98That fellow, Short, [...] will expect his full whack, I suppose\xe2\x80\x99.
1832 \xe2\x80\x98Battle of the Pigs\xe2\x80\x99 in Quid 170: Each young grunter guzzled up his whack.
1842Egan \xe2\x80\x98By-Blow of the Jug\xe2\x80\x99 in Farmer Musa Pedestris (1896) 145: And ever anxious to get his whack \xe2\x80\x93 / When scarcely ripe, he went on the crack.
1845Nat. Police Gaz. (NY) 22 Nov. 105/2: With a praiseworthy generosity of disposition, they both agreed to let him have a \xe2\x80\x98whack in\xe2\x80\x99.
1847Bell\xe2\x80\x99s Life in Sydney 18 Sept. 3: James Tait [...] was to receive one guinea as his whack.
1852Kendal Mercury 17 Apr. 6/1: The coves says there\xe2\x80\x99s a good time coming, and ven it does come, ve vill likely cop our whacks of it.
1859, 1860Hotten Dict. of Modern Sl. etc.
1866letter in Bendigo Advertiser (Vic.) 12 Oct. 2/6: [T]he girls can come down and see the sweet stuff tucked in. I suppose the fellows would have the decency to give the petticoats their whack.
1875 \xe2\x80\x98Convict Phelosiphy\xe2\x80\x99 in Stewart & Keesing (1957) 4: My name is poor Jack. / I done my whack / In sin and disepation.
1882Nat. Police Gaz. (NY) 8 Apr. 2/1: The clergymen having had their little whack at vice, the old family physicians are now coming to the front [...] with outrages on women, cases of malpractice and varied deviltries.
1888S.O. Addy Sheffield Gloss. 279: Whack, a share.
1889Dead Bird (Sydney) 16 Nov. 4/3: Takin\xe2\x80\x99 it all through I ain\xe2\x80\x99t done moren my wack [i.e in prison].
1895Derby Mercury 9 Jan. 8/3: Well we goes into a pub, and Selby gives me my whack.
1895Crowe Aus. Sl. Dict. 94: Whack, a share of the plunder.
1899C.J.C. Hyne Further Adventures of Captain Kettle 297: You go and do your whack at getting the people off that packet.
1904Sun. Times (Perth) 31 Jan. 11/4: [He] will probably cut up for a bigger whack than his deceased friend.
1905Sporting Times 4 Mar. 2/3: In these days, Alf, I notice you\xe2\x80\x99ve got to be satisfied with yer proper whack, whether you\xe2\x80\x99re a Great White Czar or only a pleadin\xe2\x80\x99 cat.
1919\xe2\x80\x98Sapper\xe2\x80\x99 Mufti 64: I say, old Side-whiskers hasn\xe2\x80\x99t given me my fair whack of beer.
1927D. Hammett \xe2\x80\x98The Big Knockover\xe2\x80\x99 Story Omnibus (1966) 310: When we find the pay-off\xe2\x80\x99s a bust, I said to the kid I was training with, \xe2\x80\x98Never mind, Kid, we\xe2\x80\x99ll get our whack.\xe2\x80\x99.
1937J. Curtis There Ain\xe2\x80\x99t No Justice 37: \xe2\x80\x98I never give you your whack, did I?\xe2\x80\x99 He handed the old man a couple of half-crowns.
1938X. Herbert Capricornia (1939) 88\xe2\x80\x939: But O\xe2\x80\x99Cannon\xe2\x80\x99s a taxpayer. He pays his whack towards the upkeep of the State School.
1940S. Lister Mistral Hotel (1951) 208: I shall take up a gun and do my whack.
1950\xe2\x80\x98Neville Shute\xe2\x80\x99 Town Like Alice 225: All right, Mrs. Boong, we\xe2\x80\x99ll each pay our own whack.
1959F. Norman Fings I i: I\xe2\x80\x99ve got ter bung the law their wack tonight an\xe2\x80\x99 all.
1966T. Keyes All Night Stand 178: Just doing a half-hour show and getting some fat whack of money for it.
1968J. Alard He who Shoots Last 4: Give me half ya wack.
1970G.F. Newman Sir, You Bastard 101: He wondered what [...] Scotty\xe2\x80\x99s whack was.
1972J. McNeill Old Familiar Juice (1973) 80: bulla: Every man has ter get his right whack, that\xe2\x80\x99s Democracy, that is!

2020G. Armstrong Young Team 126: \xe2\x80\x98A\xe2\x80\x99ll help yees the night but that\xe2\x80\x99s yir whack\xe2\x80\x99.

2. a swig of a drink, a gulp of food.

1821Egan Life in London (1869) 246: [note] The vulgar phrase of \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99ll have my whack, if it makes me sick!\xe2\x80\x99 is in most of the above instances completely verified.
c.1865 \xe2\x80\x98Funny He-She Ladies!\xe2\x80\x99 in C. Hindley Curiosities of Street Lit. (1871) 157: On skilly and whack they might regale, / Those beautiful he-she ladies.
1876Besant & Rice Golden Butterfly I 95: He was accustomed to call at the house every day about noon, accompanied by two gentlemen, who had their little whack. [...] \xe2\x80\x98Little whack!\xe2\x80\x99 thought Phyllis. \xe2\x80\x98Little glass!\xe2\x80\x99.
1897W.S. Maugham Liza of Lambeth (1966) 36: \xe2\x80\x99Urry up an\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x99ave your whack.
1912J. London Smoke Bellew (1926) 209: If you don\xe2\x80\x99t mind, Mrs Peabody, I\xe2\x80\x99ll have another whack at that steak. Make it thick and not so well done.
1922\xe2\x80\x98Sapper\xe2\x80\x99 Black Gang 291: I was beginning to feel I could do with another whack myself.
1944O. Ferguson \xe2\x80\x98Vocabulary for Lakes, [etc.]\xe2\x80\x99 AS XIX:2 105: On whack means on rations, particularly the rations prescribed as minimum in the shipping articles.
1973D. Ponicsan Cinderella Liberty 159: Forshay guzzles another whack of it.
1999N. Cohn Yes We Have No 99: She [...] takes a whack at the rum.
2013K. Sampson Killing Pool 43: I light up and drag down a whole whack of thick spicy cigar smoke and hold it in.

3. (Aus./US) a bargain.

1862Golden Age (Queenbeyan, NSW) 28 Aug. 3/4: He named a price for his steed, and on our declining to say, its \xe2\x80\x98a whack,\xe2\x80\x99 [...] looked at us reproachfully, adding that it hurt his feelings that we should refuse to trade.
1883J. Hay Bread-Winners (1884) 142: Say the word and it\xe2\x80\x99s a whack.

4. a \xe2\x80\x98go\xe2\x80\x99, a time.

1878F.H. Hart Sazerac Lying Club 59: Pard, it\xe2\x80\x99s a whack!
1885Bulletin (Sydney) 6 June 26/2: St. Peter: […] \xe2\x80\x98Why, just look, Michael, here\xe2\x80\x99s a go! / Our luck is coming back; / Geewilikins! why this is good \xe2\x80\x93 / Two bishops at a whack!\xe2\x80\x99.
1890Sporting Times 8 Mar. 2/1: We perceived that our stake was \xe2\x80\x98a sleeper,\xe2\x80\x99 that is, the croupier had been too occupied to collect it, and so it was left down for the next whack, which was to be the last.
1900Century mag. (N.Y.) Nov. 60: Lucky whack it was for me that I got here to-day, and in time to save the mine! [DA].
1910O. Johnson Varmint 183: We thought we\xe2\x80\x99d give you first whack at it.
1919A. Baer Metropolitan Hotels 1 Jan. [synd. col.] Rip [van Winkle] knocked his snooze marathon off at one whack.
1935 in G. Legman Limerick (1953) 193: For two-bits a whack / She\xe2\x80\x99d lay on her back, / And let the cowboys ram it up her crack.
c.1940 in E. Cray Erotic Muse (1992) 215: There once was an Indian maid / Who was a whore by trade. / For a dime at a whack, / She\xe2\x80\x99d lie on her back / And let the cowboys shove it up her crack.
1953R. Chandler Long Good-Bye 272: \xe2\x80\x98Prescribed dose would be one or two fifty-milligram tablets. Three or four during a twenty-four period would be the most I\xe2\x80\x99d permit.\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98But you gave her fifty at a whack,\xe2\x80\x99 Captain Hernandez said.
1967(con. 1950s) McAleer & Dickson Unit Pride (1981) 71: I\xe2\x80\x99ll take first whack at her.
2004(con. 1980) N. \xe2\x80\x98Razor\xe2\x80\x99 Smith A Few Kind Words and a Loaded Gun 235: Harry got sentenced to a fresh whack of borstal.

5. (US) a try, an attempt.

1889Wallace Co. Register (KS) 2 Nov. 3/4: Hugh Graham, too, has drifted back / [...] /To try for office another whack.
1899Ade Fables in Sl. (1902) 118: He looked to her to be a Coming Congressman and she wanted to get a Whack at Washington Society.
1915S. Ford Torchy, Private Sec. 11: Much obliged to you, Mr. Rowley, for givin\xe2\x80\x99 us first whack at it.
1934M. Fulcher \xe2\x80\x98Believe Me\xe2\x80\x99 in Afro-American (Baltimore, MD) 1 Sept. 12/3: I\xe2\x80\x99ve been aching for a whack at Senor Malcolm\xe2\x80\x99s column.
1957W.R. Burnett Underdog 110: I\xe2\x80\x99ve stayed away from the big game [i.e. high-stakes craps] since we got married [...] Now I\xe2\x80\x99d like a whack at it again.
1970San Diego Sailor 54: I wanted to get some rest so that I\xe2\x80\x99d been in shape for another whack at it [i.e. intercourse] later on.
1971Wodehouse Much Obliged, Jeeves 35: I was in merry mood [...] as always when about to get another whack at Anatole\xe2\x80\x99s cooking.
1995C. Hiaasen Stormy Weather 5: Not that Edie wouldn\xe2\x80\x99t have crawled nude across broken glass for a whack at John Jr.

6. (UK milit.) a period of enlistment.

1896Regiment 25 July 263/1: \xe2\x80\x98Thank God, my whack is nearly done,\xe2\x80\x99 I have \xe2\x80\x98heard many a Non-Commissioned Officer and Man remark, when perhaps they have had a \xe2\x80\x98wheel\xe2\x80\x99 or the Sergeant-Major has been down on them.

7. (UK und.) a rented room, i.e. a share of a house.

1935Thieves Slang ms list from District Police Training Centre, Ryton-on-Dunsmore, Warwicks 4: Furnished whack: Furnished rooms.

8. a range, a selection.

2012(con. 1943) G.S. Manson Coorparoo Blues [ebook] \xe2\x80\x98[B]loody scads of it [i.e. medical supplies]: bandages, saline, chloroform, morphine, the whole wack\xe2\x80\x99.

9. (US) an aspect.

1957\xe2\x80\x98Ed Lacy\xe2\x80\x99 Lead With Your Left (1958) 89: I\xe2\x80\x99ve never been able to figure if the slang name [i.e. \xe2\x80\x98rabbi\xe2\x80\x99 = influential individual] has an anti-Semitic wack or not.

10. a large sum of money.

1988R. Doyle Commitments 25: Five pounds, three and six pence. A fair whack in those days.
1995Francis-Jackson Official Dancehall Dict. 54: Wack a large sum of money.
2021C. Hammer Opal Country 213: The first thing he sees is the huge whack of money [...] $287,000.

11. (drugs) a portion of a drug, e.g. a \xe2\x80\x98line\xe2\x80\x99 of a narcotic, a puff of cannabis.

1943W.R. Burnett Nobody Lives for Ever 28: His face got very pale. An almost unbearable depression settled over him. He needed a whack of the stuff.
1998I. Welsh Filth 86: I take a whack, and it is good stuff [...] a surge of power flowing through me.
2001ONDCP Street Terms 22: Whack \xe2\x80\x94 Crack Cocaine; heroin and PCP; Crack/PCP mixture or marijuana laced with insecticides.
c.2003D. Vrij \xe2\x80\x98Tying Off\xe2\x80\x99 on Inter-zone.org By day, and into the evening, heroin and powder cocaine were cheap and abundant, the $15 smack quarters, were fatter than the downtown 20s, all night long the flailers, could pick up on whacks of speed.

12. (Aus.) a bet.

1996P. Temple Bad Debts (2012) [ebook] It\xe2\x80\x99s getting hard to find someone in the bush\xe2\x80\x99ll take a decent-size whack.

13. income.

2002J. Cameron Hell on Hoe Street 107: Got to be reckoning it just a faint possibility he never got his Volvo [...] off his inspector\xe2\x80\x99s whack.
