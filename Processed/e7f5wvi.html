jack n.1

[later 20C+ usage is US black; given Shakespearian take down, to reduce a man\xe2\x80\x99s sexual desire, cit. at 1595 may refer to jack n.3 (1)]

c.1495Skelton Agaynste A Comsely Coystrowne i line 14: Lo, Jak wold be a jentyl man! [...] An ussher of the hall fayn wold I get To poynyte this proude page a place and a rome, For Jak wold be a jentylman, that late was a grome.

5. (Anglo-Ind., also Jack Sepoy) a sepoy.

6. (US, \n also country jack) a rustic, a peasant; a simpleton [underpinned by jake n.1 (1)].
