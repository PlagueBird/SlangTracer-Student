cop adj.

[cop n.1 (1)]

pertaining to the police or police culture.

1945R.L. Bellem \xe2\x80\x98Ruby Ransom\xe2\x80\x99 Hollywood Detective Dec. Nabbing burglars is a cop job.
1953W.P. McGivern Big Heat 67: He was acting queerly, ignoring some basic cop-rules.
1970E.K. Walker \xe2\x80\x98Harlem Transfer\xe2\x80\x99 in King Black Short Story Anthol. (1972) 50: See that cop captain up there.
1977 in K. Gilbert Living Black 30: As soon as they\xe2\x80\x99ve seen the cop car, others are dashing over to report about so \xe2\x80\x99n so.
2004L. Redhead Peepshow [ebook] My 1967 Ford Futura shuddered to a halt next to the cop car.
2012 G. Johnstone \xe2\x80\x98No Through Road\xe2\x80\x99 in Crime Factory: Hard Labour [ebook] The sight of a cop car or paddy wagon bringing him home [...] was a regular occurrence.
