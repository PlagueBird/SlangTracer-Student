stiff v.1

[? the response, \xe2\x80\x98I say, that\xe2\x80\x99s a bit stiff,\xe2\x80\x99 to such an outburst]

to curse, to swear.

1888Kipling \xe2\x80\x98The Arrest of Lieutenant Golightly\xe2\x80\x99 in Plain Tales from the Hills (1890) 131: I\xe2\x80\x99ve \xe2\x80\x99eard a few beggars in the clink blind, stiff and crack on a bit.
1949Wodehouse Mating Season 98: Nor could one fairly blame them for blinding and stiffing.
1960Wodehouse Jeeves in the Offing 33: Those solid citizens have to learn to curb the tongue. Creates a bad impression, I mean, if they start blinding and stiffing.
