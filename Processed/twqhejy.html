chip n.1

[SE colloq. phr. chip off the old block]

1. a child.

c.1698B.E. Dict. Canting Crew.
1785, 1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1861(con. 1840s\xe2\x80\x9350s) H. Mayhew London Labour and London Poor III 126/1: \xe2\x80\x98Yes [...] there\xe2\x80\x99s a chip of mine will sing it for me,\xe2\x80\x99 and I introduce my little boy.
1897E.W. Townsend Chimmie Fadden 36: Dere is a little chip [...] what dusts rooms, and tings like dat.
1900Marvel 8 Dec. 27: When they tumble to it that there\xe2\x80\x99s a chip of Old Man Bennett knocking around.

2. a man, a fellow.

1827Exeter & Plymouth Gaz. 8 Mar. 5/2: Yes; Peg\xe2\x80\x99s a queer old \xe2\x80\x98chip\xe2\x80\x99 d\xe2\x80\x99ye see.
