queer n.

[SE queer, odd/queer adj.]

1. a hoax, a confidence trick; thus play the queer v., to hoodwink.

1754Scoundrel\xe2\x80\x99s Dict. 15: Cunning \xe2\x80\x93 Queer.
1830Lytton Paul Clifford II 265: There\xe2\x80\x99s some swell cove of a lord gives a blow-out to-day; and the lads, dear souls! think to play the queer on some straggler.

1821Egan Life in London (1869) 193: The Duke and the \xe2\x80\x98Dealer in Queer\xe2\x80\x99.
1836A. Thornton Don Juan in London II 406: That Jew is a dealer in base coin. [...] Regular markets in various public and private houses are kept by the principal agents, who receive the white and yellow queer, as it is called.

3. soot; thus dealer in queer, a chimney sweep.

1819P. Egan Key to the Picture of the Fancy going to a Fight 20: [W]hy should not the Flue Faker enjoy the pleasures of a mill [...] \xe2\x80\x98Vy,\xe2\x80\x99 says the dealer in queer [...] \xe2\x80\x98I does the best to arn an honest penny\xe2\x80\x99.

4. a look (on one\xe2\x80\x99s face); a look (at something).

1880Henley & Stevenson Deacon Brodie I tab.I v: Won\xe2\x80\x99t Mrs. Deacon let me have a queer at her phiz?

7. (US Und.) a heterosexual who enjoys non-standard sexual practices.

8. (Aus.) a fool, a simpleton.

9. (US campus) one who works (overly) hard.

queerbait \n (n.)\n

an effeminate young boy who attracts, or is supposed to attract older male homosexuals; thus as v., to seek out a homosexual encounter for money.
