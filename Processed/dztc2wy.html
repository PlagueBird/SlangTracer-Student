pitch n.1

[fig. uses of SE pitch, to throw]

1. sales talk, esp. when inflated; also in fig. use.

1846Swell\xe2\x80\x99s Night Guide 51: Fuzzy had got his pecker up, and was stalling up to Sall, but she cut his pitch.
c.1864A. Vance Chickaleary Cove 3: At Groves\xe2\x80\x99s you\xe2\x80\x99re safe to make a sure pitch [F&H].
1876C. Hindley Life and Adventures of a Cheap Jack 63: We tossed who should make the first pitch.
1885Bulletin (Sydney) 21 Feb. 10/2: A bevy of clerical gentlemen were \xe2\x80\x98doing a pitch\xe2\x80\x99 lately on the subject of local option and the liquor question generally.
1908A.N. Lyons Arthur\xe2\x80\x99s 17: A Creepin\xe2\x80\x99-Jenny beard like that would spoil the softest pitch.
1914Jackson & Hellyer Vocab. Criminal Sl. 65: pitch [...] the term used by street fakirs to describe the operation of beguiling the public from a soap box, a platform, a carriage or automobile selling merchandise from an eminence like an auctioneer.
1947B. Schulberg Harder They Fall (1971) 90: I felt I had to make a pitch in the right direction.
1955B. Schulberg On the Waterfront (1964) 170: Father Barry\xe2\x80\x99s pitch had been to urge the boys to co-operate with the Crime Commission.
1960H.S. Thompson letter 19 Oct. in Proud Highway (1997) 233: I have applied for jobs [...] and made countless pitches of the \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99ll do anything for money\xe2\x80\x99 variety.
1977D. Powis Signs of Crime 196: Pitch (a) Sales talk.
1986J. Ellroy Suicide Hill 71: \xe2\x80\x98Then here's the pitch: [...] If you don't do what I want. my buddies chop Sally to pieces\xe2\x80\x99.
1989J. Morton Lowspeak.
1998L. Stringer Grand Central Winter (1999) 181: I do my pitch and it falls flat.
2000W. Shaw Westsiders 209: Goldsby [...] is won over by Babyboy\xe2\x80\x99s pitch.
2012W. Keyser \xe2\x80\x98Carny Lingo\xe2\x80\x99 in http://goodmagic.com Now you have a \xe2\x80\x98tip,\xe2\x80\x99 and it\xe2\x80\x99s time to give them \xe2\x80\x98the pitch,\xe2\x80\x99 the part where you describe in glowing hyperbole the glories to be seen inside.

2. (orig. US) any plan that should benefit its maker, a scheme, esp. a piece of trickery or deceit.

1865Leaves from Diary of Celebrated Burglar 148/1: The porter had heard something of the affair [...] probably thinking we would never return for what we had left, and in that case our property would fall into his hands [...] but our appearance put a \xe2\x80\x98crab\xe2\x80\x99 on that \xe2\x80\x98pitch\xe2\x80\x99.
1914E. Dyson Spats\xe2\x80\x99 Fact\xe2\x80\x99ry (1922) 74: Then this accounts fer yer comin\xe2\x80\x99 into money. But how-jer strike the pitch!
1940J.H. O\xe2\x80\x99Hara Pal Joey 46: I never made a pitch with Herta.
1949W.R. Burnett Asphalt Jungle in Four Novels (1984) 178: What\xe2\x80\x99s the pitch?
1951Kerouac On the Road (The Orig. Scroll) (2007) 314: \xe2\x80\x98And what\xe2\x80\x99s the pitch?\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98No pitch man, I only want to know what\xe2\x80\x99s been happening.\xe2\x80\x99.
1965R.L. Pike Mute Witness (1997) 48: Well, that\xe2\x80\x99s the pitch.
1974V.E. Smith Jones Men 8: Bennie Lee had made a pitch and learned his lesson the hard way.

3. a conversation, a chat.

1897T.J. Henry Claude Garton 58: Mostyn [...] frequently called on Claude at his rooms for a yarn\xe2\x80\x94a pitch he called it.
1900Bulletin (Sydney) 7 July 14/4: Met \xe2\x80\x99im myself one day, an\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x99ad a bit of a \xe2\x80\x98pitch\xe2\x80\x99 ter \xe2\x80\x99im, an\xe2\x80\x99 after \xe2\x80\x99e\xe2\x80\x99d bin spoutin\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x99bout ten minutes I says ter \xe2\x80\x99im, \xe2\x80\x98Ain\xe2\x80\x99t yer goin\xe2\x80\x99 ter put ther flour down fer a bit?\xe2\x80\x99.
c.1905J. Furphy Buln-Buln and the Brolga (1948) I\xe2\x80\x99d like to have a pitch with you \xe2\x80\x93 sposen I wouldn\xe2\x80\x99t be in your road .
1915Bulletin (Sydney) 11 Dec. 51/2: He turned in at Casey\xe2\x80\x99s pub and had a pitch with the blonde barmaid.

4. the line of talk used by a swindler.

1905Little Falls Herald (MN) 31 Mar. 3/3: How to Operate the Shell Game with Profit [...] Make a hot lamas before the rube gets the green in his mit, and do a ringer before making another pitch.
1932Sun. Mail (Brisbane) 13 Nov. 20/8: \xe2\x80\x98Hoons\xe2\x80\x99 is the technical term for hoodlums; a \xe2\x80\x98pitch\xe2\x80\x99 is what the \xe2\x80\x98whizz-men\xe2\x80\x99 call a trick .
1934G. & S. Lorimer Stag Line 161: You gotta have a pile of pitch for the come-on, see?
1957R. Prather Always Leave \xe2\x80\x99Em Dying 69: What\xe2\x80\x99s the pitch, Randy? I\xe2\x80\x99ve never heard him in action. He found a new way to get to heaven, or what?
1963 in T.I. Rubin Sweet Daddy 22: You know the pitch, Doc. Paid her but she would have done it for free, she liked my action so much. What crap!

5. an area conducive to crime.

1941V. Davis Phenomena in Crime 87: His best \xe2\x80\x98pitch\xe2\x80\x99 for cocaine and heroin.
1977D. Powis Signs of Crime 196: Pitch [...] (b) street site used by pavement salesmen or three-card trick men, (c) a prostitute\xe2\x80\x99s territory.

6. (US) a situation.

1946W.P. McGivern \xe2\x80\x98Manchu Terror\xe2\x80\x99 in Goodstone Pulps (1970) 24/1: What\xe2\x80\x99s the pitch? The guy looks at me like he knew me before somewhere.
1951M. Spillane Long Wait (1954) 22: A lot of things happen in five years. What\xe2\x80\x99s the pitch?
1963M. Spillane Return of the Hood 10: By then Wally Pee and Izzy Goldswitz had caught the pitch and were begging me with their eyes to get the hell out.
1974G.F. Newman You Flash Bastard 27: Those arrests and convictions [...] might have been the zenith of an ephemeral career, and he wondered what could follow that might achieve a worthwhile pitch.
