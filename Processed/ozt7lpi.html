do v.2

1. to inspect as a tourist, to visit, e.g. do London.

1865G.A. Sala My Diary in America I 139: One can \xe2\x80\x98do\xe2\x80\x99 Niagara without penetrating into the British provinces.
1868H.L. Williams Black-Eyed Beauty 46: If ever I raise a pile big enough, I\xe2\x80\x99ll do London as a tip-top figure.
1875\xe2\x80\x98Aliph Cheem\xe2\x80\x99 Lays of Ind (1905) 68: He did the theatres, music halls, the Crystal Palace oh! / [...] / He also did the Park.
1878G. Leybourne \xe2\x80\x98Parisien Harry\xe2\x80\x99 Comic Songs 9: Some fellers do the Rhine.
