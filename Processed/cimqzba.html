chop n.1

1. a blow with the fist, esp. to the face.

1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue (2nd, 3rd edn).
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1943M. Harrison Reported Safe Arrival 65: That bloke\xe2\x80\x99s arskin\xe2\x80\x99 fer a chop rahnd the ear\xe2\x80\x99oles.

2. (UK Und.) a bargain based on exchange of goods.

1876C. Hindley Life and Adventures of a Cheap Jack 141: We came to terms for another \xe2\x80\x98chop\xe2\x80\x99.

3. (orig. Aus.) a share, portion [chop v.5 ].

1917\xe2\x80\x98Ian Hay\xe2\x80\x99 Carrying On 176: You shall have first chop at the Victoria crosses.
1924(con. WWI) A.G. Pretty Gloss. of Sl. [...] in the A.I.F. 1921\xe2\x80\x931924 (rev. t/s) n.p.: chop. Share. \xe2\x80\x98To hop in for ones [sic] chop,\xe2\x80\x99 to enter in, in order to secure a priviledge [sic] or benefit.
1934Frankston & Somerville Standard (Vic.) 7 Dec. 4/5: I am having a chop at this witness\xe2\x80\x99.
1944L. Glassop We Were the Rats 117: I calls round at the drum and gets me chop from them, too.
1951(con. 1941) E. Lambert Twenty Thousand Thieves 189: If we get our extra beer I bet you hop in for your chop.
1968A. Buzo Norm and Ahmed (1973) 18: In this world there\xe2\x80\x99s too many blokes getting in for their chop and not worrying about their mates.
1988McGill Dict. of Kiwi Sl. 27/2: chop share or cut.
1998Bug (Aus.) 28 Jan. If the club\xe2\x80\x99s prepared for that big a blowout, it would be selfish of Wendell not to get in for his chop while the money lasts.
2003McGill Reed Dict. of N.Z. Sl. [as cit. 1988].

4. (Aus.) money.

1933Bulletin (Sydney) 6 Sept. 41/2: I gave them each their chop and beat it for the city.
1986R.G. Barrett Real Thing 32: [He] came out with a rather fat wallet. \xe2\x80\x98Hello, what have we got here? Some chops?\xe2\x80\x99 he counted out the money.
1998R.G. Barrett Goodoo Goodoo 24: Twenty-five grand. That\xe2\x80\x99s a lot of chops.

5. (orig. US) a cut, usu. in a salary or in a price.

1942Berrey & Van den Bark Amer. Thes. Sl.
1977(con. 1941) R. Beilby Gunner 296: It had been raining tails! All afternoon there hadn\xe2\x80\x99t been more than three heads spun [...] \xe2\x80\x98What about a bitta chop from you tailies? Come on, ya\xe2\x80\x99ve hada good run.\xe2\x80\x99.
1995Chrysler UK Media 18 Oct. So keen was he to get into the motor industry that he even took a 50 per cent chop in salary.

6. (US juv.) an insult, a cruel remark.

1958H. Ellison Web of the City (1983) 47: It was the worst. It was a chop low like no other he\xe2\x80\x99d ever heard.
1971E.E. Landy Underground Dict. (1972) 50: chop [...] a.k.a. chop down. n. an insult.

7. punishment.

1971T. Lewis Plender [ebook] Well, I didn\xe2\x80\x99t actually kill her, but [...] I\xe2\x80\x99m for the chop just the same.
1978A. Burgess 1985 (1980) 168: This bugger\xe2\x80\x99s up for the chop.
2013K. Sampson Killing Pool 183: Now I know I\xe2\x80\x99m for the chop. \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99ve been following different leads, boys. Come on\xe2\x80\x99.

8. (drugs) the chopping of cocaine into lines.

1998I. Welsh Filth 193: He reckons it gives extra quality tae the chop.

\n In compounds\n

chop-up \n (n.)\n

(Aus.) a division of criminal plunder, or any accumulation of money.

1936Dly Mercury (Mackay, Qld) 29 May 16/1: Thev had a silly argument on Wednesday night, and a fight afterwards. If anyone said it was over a \xe2\x80\x98chop-up\xe2\x80\x99 of money he would say it was a lie.
1945Aus. Worker (Sydney) 29 Aug. 6/6: Workers in the pastoral industry will remember that every known method was used to extort money from them [...] and maybe they may have preferred a chop-up of any cash which they may have had.
1967 \xe2\x80\x98Whisper All Aussie Dict.\xe2\x80\x99 in Kings Cross Whisper (Sydney) xxxiii 4/3: chop: Share of loot, see also corner. \xe2\x80\x98How many in the \xe2\x80\x98chop-up\xe2\x80\x99?\xe2\x80\x99.

1. to be killed.

1946G. Gibson Enemy Coast Ahead (1955) 282: He got the chop.
1950P. Brickhill Great Escape 64: \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99d rather be flying again,\xe2\x80\x99 Thompson said dolefully, \xe2\x80\x98even if I did get the chop\xe2\x80\x99.
1957J. Braine Room at the Top (1959) 162: We noncoms used to say got the chopper. Going for a Burton was journalist\xe2\x80\x99s talk.
1959G. Slatter Gun in My Hand 150: Got the chop over Cologne, went down in a flamer.
1975\xe2\x80\x98P.B. Yuill\xe2\x80\x99 Hazell and the Three-card Trick (1977) 172: I stand to get the chop, doan I?
1983T. Paulin \xe2\x80\x98Yes, the Maternity Unit\xe2\x80\x99 in Liberty Tree 35: Limp king dick has got the chop / but Quim and Bum must slog it out.
1999Guardian G2 10 Sept. 19: Another bunch of innocents gets the chop.
2001M. Twight Kiss Or Kill 87: That\xe2\x80\x99s how alpine climbers get the chop.
2013K. Sampson Killing Pool 192: I\xe2\x80\x99m getting the chop. What a way to go. Slain. Bled to death on a lonely floor.

2. to be dismissed from one\xe2\x80\x99s job, or from a sports team.

1959M. Pugh Chancer 139: He hoped to have one last leave in Kashmir before he got the chopper.
1968G. Slatter Pagan Game (1969) 152: My boy got the chop.
1998A. Warner Sopranos 255: [of expulsion from school] They\xe2\x80\x99ll just get suspensions, but us cause there\xe2\x80\x99s alcohol involved we\xe2\x80\x99re for the chop.

2000Indep. Rev. 16 Mar. 11: Gary Davies heard his \xe2\x80\x98Bit in the Middle\xe2\x80\x99 was getting the chop.

2011Sowetan (Johannesburg) 24 June Once again motor-mouth Luclay seems to have survived the chop.

3. to be rejected or dismissed, to have one\xe2\x80\x99s relationship ended.

1996J. Cameron It Was An Accident 47: Came out on jam roll, got a nice new gaff courtesy of my mates, got the chop off that Kelly no bad deal.
give someone the chop \n (v.)\n

1. (also give someone the old chop-chop) to kill or otherwise dispose of a person.

1968G. Cuomo Among Thieves 473: If he had any brains he would have just shut up and waited for Verdun to give Orninski the old chop-chop and for the intrepid goon to lead the whole pack of rascals out.

2. to fire from a job.

1975A. Bleasdale Scully 150: That\xe2\x80\x99s what posh people say when they\xe2\x80\x99re going to give you the chop.
1992M. Frayn Now You Know 18: Want to know who\xe2\x80\x99s for the chop in the Beeb?
1997(con. 1950s) J. Byrne Slab Boys [film script] 91: The wee guy\xe2\x80\x99s fur the chop ... has to be.
2024J. Meades Empty Wigs (t/s) 175: The majority of the farm\xe2\x80\x99s labourers had been given the chop long since.

3. to destroy, to abandon, to stop, to cut off.

1999Sun 28 Dec. 26: His party could be for the chop.
gone for the chop \n (adj.)\n

finished, defeated, made impossible.

1972Galton & Simpson \xe2\x80\x98Oh, What a Beautiful Morning\xe2\x80\x99 Steptoe and Son [TV script] That\xe2\x80\x99s my yoga night gone for the chop.

(Aus.) to seize one\xe2\x80\x99s opportunity.

1936J. Devanny Sugar Heaven 261: You\xe2\x80\x99re in a position where you can hop in for your chop. There\xe2\x80\x99s an awful lot of people in this world who have no chop to hop in for.
1945G. Casey Downhill Is Easier 89: \xe2\x80\x98You\xe2\x80\x99ve got to hop in for your chop,\xe2\x80\x99 Eric pointed out. [...] \xe2\x80\x98You\xe2\x80\x99ve got to make hay while the sun shines\xe2\x80\x99.
1952T.A.G. Hungerford Ridge & River 212: There\xe2\x80\x99s got to be leaders, so hop in for your chop. Think of the dough and the privileges.
1954(con. 1940s) E. Lambert Veterans 16: Hop in for your chop. Make \xe2\x80\x99em give you everything you\xe2\x80\x99re entitled to.
1968P. Adam-Smith Tiger Country 70: I once saw George \xe2\x80\x98hop in for his chop.\xe2\x80\x99 It was at the Royal Hobart Show in 1960. He'd slashed his shin open with an axe in the bush only a week before but still came in to compete .

(Aus./N.Z.) out for oneself, for one\xe2\x80\x99s own profit or advantage.
