bumper n.1

1. (UK Und.) a pickpocket\xe2\x80\x99s assistant who bumps into the victim.

1943Baker Popular Dict. Aus. Sl. (2nd edn).

2. a thug, a pimp.

1939J. Worby Spiv\xe2\x80\x99s Progress 120: Rosy\xe2\x80\x99s a bad un - she and her black bumper .
1946D. Burley N.Y. Amsterdam News 14 Dec. 21: Big Freddie, the Bumper [...] the No Good man.

3. (Irish) an amateur flat race [? contestants may fall off with a bump or bump each other\xe2\x80\x99s horses].

1990R. Smith Vincent O\xe2\x80\x99Brien, The Master of Ballydoyle n.p.: The race that he chose for his first gamble was the Sportman\xe2\x80\x99s Plate, an amateur flat race \xe2\x80\x93 or a \xe2\x80\x98bumper\xe2\x80\x99, as styled in Ireland [BS].
1996Leinster Leader (Naas, County Kildare) 24 Oct. n.p.: Bumpers are very popular with the betting public [BS].
