canary v.

1. (US) to sing.

1905F. Hutchison Philosophy of Johnny the Gent 22: Them guys that separate themselves from a five case note to [...] hear a mob o\xe2\x80\x99 Dagos canary.

2. of a criminal, to confess, to turn state\xe2\x80\x99s evidence [canary n.1 (4c)].

1949Monteleone Criminal Sl. (rev. edn).

3. (US) to work as a band vocalist [canary n.1 (4e)].

1938W. Winchell On Broadway 24 Nov. [synd. col.] Ross Maclean\xe2\x80\x99s canarying at the New Yorker.
