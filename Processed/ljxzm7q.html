bunny n.1

[SE bunny, an affectionate name for both rabbits and squirrels]

1. in sexual contexts.

(a) the vagina.

[1601 Percy Cuck-Queanes 26: Some hauing espide a Bunnes neast, in the windowe].
1720 in D\xe2\x80\x99Urfey Pills to Purge Melancholy VI 325: Old must Maids that have Money, / Although no Teeth in their Heads; / Must have a Bit for their Bunny, / To pleasure them in their beds.

(c) (US black) a promiscuous woman, whose habits emulate the stereotyped preoccupations of rabbits.

(f) (US) the buttocks, the anus.

2. (US campus) rarebit.

1893W.K. Post Harvard Stories 102: \xe2\x80\x98Aha,\xe2\x80\x99 cried Stoughton, who was stirring the \xe2\x80\x98bunny\xe2\x80\x99 with a master hand. [...] While they were eating the rarebit, a step was heard in the entry.

(d) (US black) a weakling.
