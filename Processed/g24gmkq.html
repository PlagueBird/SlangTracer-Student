tip n.3

[ety. unknown]

1. (orig. US Und.) a crowd of people, an audience.

1866implied in working the tip
1933Ersine Und. and Prison Sl.
1940D. Maurer Big Con 310: TIP. A crowd of people.
1949F. Brown Dead Ringer 45: There was a big tip in front of the posing show.
1949Monteleone Criminal Sl. (rev. edn).
1953F. Brown Madball (2019) 82: Not a big tip and mostly kids, [...] and if he stalled any longer some of them would start wandering off.
1962C. Clausen I Love You Honey, But the Season\xe2\x80\x99s Over 173: He also offered hot soup as a remedy for [...] a \xe2\x80\x98bad tip\xe2\x80\x99 (bad crowd) on the midway.
1972N. Algren \xe2\x80\x98The Last Carousel\xe2\x80\x99 in Texas Stories (1995) 149: Ten o\xe2\x80\x99clock, the hour when the tip begins milling from bally to bally, trying to see everything before midnight.
2012W. Keyser \xe2\x80\x98Carny Lingo\xe2\x80\x99 in http://goodmagic.com You\xe2\x80\x99ve assembled a gaggle of freeloaders, but they're not a \xe2\x80\x98tip\xe2\x80\x99 until they're paying close and continued attention. \xe2\x80\x98Freezing the tip\xe2\x80\x99 is getting them almost immobilized...get them to move closer to see better, making it difficult for anyone to leave .

2. (US Und.) a prison gang.

1990S. Morgan Homeboy 152: Every joint had its tips for controlling dope and prostitution and gambling.
2004D. Grann \xe2\x80\x98The Brand\xe2\x80\x99 in New Yorker 16 Feb. 158/3: While there had always been cliques in prison, known as \xe2\x80\x98tips,\xe2\x80\x99 these men were now aligned by race and resorted to a kind of violence that had never been seen at San Quentin.
