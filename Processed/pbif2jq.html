rattle n.

1. in senses of speech.

(a) the tongue, the voice; thus noise.

a.1649Hakewell Apology n.p.: All this ado about the golden age, is but an empty rattle and frivolous conceit [F&H].
1663J. Wilson Cheats V v: He has got a rattle as big as a drum.
1707Humours of a Coffee-House 2 July 9: Mr Harlem is not here with his Nonsensical Rattle to interrupt us.
1711Swift letter lx 24 Feb. in Journal to Stella (1901) 513: I chid the servants, and made a rattle.
1779Nocturnal Revels I 200: The rattle of riotous Rakes.
1780Mme D\xe2\x80\x99Arblay Diary (1891) 1 271: The careless rattle of Captain Bouchier, which paid no regard to the daintiness of Miss Weston, made her [...] laugh.
1832Egan Bk of Sports 154: B\xe2\x80\x94 st you, if you don\xe2\x80\x99t hold your rattle.
1848Thackeray Vanity Fair III 128: The boy\xe2\x80\x99s dashing manners, and off-hand rattle about books.
1948L.T.C. Rolt Sleep No More (1994) 92: \xe2\x80\x98\xe2\x80\x99Old tha rattle.\xe2\x80\x99 he said, giving the last speaker a queer look.
2001A. Sillitoe Birthday 90: I\xe2\x80\x99ll put my boot in your big mouth if you don\xe2\x80\x99t shut your rattle.

(b) a voluble talker.

1715\xe2\x80\x98Nickydemus Ninnyhammer\xe2\x80\x99 Homer in a nut-shell 32: He\xe2\x80\x99s always an eternal Rattle, / Will never flinch at Verbal Battle .
1827W.T. Moncrieff Bashful Man I iii: What a rattle you are, Frank!
1870Cythera\xe2\x80\x99s Hymnal 48: When taken down to dinner by some brisk rattle, / Who of music, novels and plays will prattlke.
1885Bulletin (Sydney) 6 June 6/4: There is just as much Jew in the one as there is jewellery about the other, but they are both agreeable [?]attles.
1890Bird o\xe2\x80\x99 Freedom 8 Jan. 5/4: She is a rattle, but an undoubtably agreeable rattle, and she had kept her visitor amused, without a check of ennui or a call for a lead, during a fifteen minutes\xe2\x80\x99 burst of small talk.

(c) (UK Und.) chatter from a confidence trickster used to distract a victim.

1887Cincinnati Enquirer (OH) 24 Dec. 12/2: \xe2\x80\x98Give him the “rattle with your mouth all the time you\xe2\x80\x99re working him\xe2\x80\x99.

(d) (Aus./S. Afr. Und.) a dispute, a quarrel.

1963L.F. Freed Crime in S. Afr. 75: They employed an argot which was peculiarly their own [...] \xe2\x80\x98a rattle\xe2\x80\x99 meant a fight.
1986R.G. Barrett Real Thing 50: Take Les over to the car [...] I\xe2\x80\x99ll sort this rattle out.
2002R.G. Barrett Mystery Bay Blues 2: While all this rattle was going on Les had the money he had stolen washed quicker than a cup and saucer.

(e) (US prison) petty grievances.

1976M. Braly False Starts 195: He was a total prick, who would beef you for an overdue library book, that was fair enough, we knew him by his rattles and behaved accordingly.

(f) (Aus.) distraction, unwanted noise.

1989R.G. Barratt \xe2\x80\x98So Why Doesn\xe2\x80\x99t Jack the Lad Get a Real Job?\xe2\x80\x99 in What Do You Reckon (1997) [ebook] Amidst all this rattle you try to write a book.

(g) (Aus.) publicity.

1992R.G. Barrett White Shoes 10: I\xe2\x80\x99ve got Crystal promoting her new single [...] I\xe2\x80\x99m doing the whole rattle on the Gold Coast.

(h) (Aus.) some form of (energetic) activity.

1998R.G. Barrett Mud Crab Boogie (2013) [ebook] All I have to do now is get the tiler back when this rattle with Nizegy\xe2\x80\x99s all over.
2000R.G. Barrett Leaving Bondi (2013) [ebook] While allthis rattle was going on, another scene of organised confusion was being enacted next to the children\xe2\x80\x99s play station.

2. a dicebox.

c.1786Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue ms. additions n.p.: Rattle. a Dice Box.
1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue (2nd, 3rd edn).
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1823Egan Grose\xe2\x80\x99s Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.

3. a coach.

1791 \xe2\x80\x98Flash Lang.\xe2\x80\x99 in Confessions of Thomas Mount 19: A coach, a rattle.
1791H. Lemoine \xe2\x80\x98Education\xe2\x80\x99 in Attic Misc. 117: While in a rattle sit two blowens flash, / Salt tears fast streaming from each bungy eye.
1820Jack Randall\xe2\x80\x99s Diary 3: Tom Trot\xe2\x80\x99s bran new Rattle, From Tothill Fields came trotting down.
1824 \xe2\x80\x98Sonnets for the Fancy\xe2\x80\x99 in Egan Boxiana III 622: [as 1791].
1895C. Crowe Aus. Sl. Dict. Rattle, a coach; rattling cove, a coachman: .

4. in pl. uses [abbr. SE death-rattle].

(a) the croup.

1744J. Hempstead Diary 434: A Child [...] died of the Rattles or Throat Destemper [DA].
1806W. Pettigrew Letters 26 Nov. [ms] He was then taken very bad with the rattels [DA].

(b) nerves, anxiety, esp. as case of (the) rattles.

1845W.T. Porter Big Bear of Arkansas (1847) 121: They jist kept Chunkey from dyin\xe2\x80\x99, as he was so dry he had the rattles.
1887World (N.Y.) 2 Sept. 6/6: The muff and the subsequent \xe2\x80\x98case of rattles\xe2\x80\x99 which the young player had, caused the loss for New York.
1897E.W. Townsend Chimmie Fadden 70: I nearly got de rattles once, and was going t\xe2\x80\x99 make de grand sneak.
1905\xe2\x80\x98Hugh McHugh\xe2\x80\x99 You Can Search Me 12: It isn\xe2\x80\x99t a case of the rattles with me.

5. money, cash; thus have a bit of rattle v., to be well-off.

1848J.R. Lowell Biglow Papers (1880) 80: Ez long\xe2\x80\x99z the people get their rattle, / Wut is there fer \xe2\x80\x99m to grout about?
1929(con. 1916) F. Manning Her Privates We (1986) 103: I should say the old girl \xe2\x80\x99ad quite a bit o\xe2\x80\x99 rattle to \xe2\x80\x99er.

6. spirit, ebullience.

1879 \xe2\x80\x98\xe2\x80\x99Arry in Parry\xe2\x80\x99 in Punch 15 Nov. 217/2: I put on the rattle to rights in the style that\xe2\x80\x99s so taking shay noo.
1895 \xe2\x80\x98\xe2\x80\x99Arry on Harry\xe2\x80\x99\xe2\x80\x99 in Punch 24 Aug. 93/2: But there\xe2\x80\x99s one thing [...] you carn\xe2\x80\x99t borrer, and that\xe2\x80\x99s my rattle and go.

7. an opportunity, a chance [play on sense 2].

1938D. Runyon \xe2\x80\x98Big Shoulders\xe2\x80\x99 in Runyon on Broadway (1954) 584: They are the only propositions that give you a dead-square rattle.
1944D. Runyon Runyon à la Carte 72: I figure that with any kind of a square rattle I will have a better chance of nailing him than he has of nailing me.

8. an act of sexual intercourse [note the double entendre in Burns c.1800: \xe2\x80\x98But it\xe2\x80\x99s in among the blankets that I like best, / To get a jolly rattle at the cuckoo\xe2\x80\x99s nest\xe2\x80\x99].

[1786 \xe2\x80\x98The Court of Equity\xe2\x80\x99 in Burns Merry Muses of Caledonia (1965) 212: An\xe2\x80\x99 gied her canister a rattle].
1963P. O\xe2\x80\x99Hara Red Sailor 12: I trunked her in the doorway [...] Not a bad rattle either, mate.
1966Trimble 5000 Adult Sex Words and Phrases.
2005P. Howard Curious Incident of the Dog in the Nightdress 110: He knows that I gave Jessica an old rattle.

9. (UK drugs) withdrawal from narcotics addiction [the aches and pains that rattle the body].

2004Observer 25 Apr. 11/2: Last time I came in [...] I had a straight rattle [cold turkey] and I got really depressed. [...] Coming off heroin is just too hard.

rattle-box \n (n.)\n

1. a factory.

1846Northern Star 7 Feb. 13/5: The man who rushes, maddened with toil, and addled with noise, from the \xe2\x80\x98rattle-box\xe2\x80\x99 to the gin palace.
1849Northern Star 10 Nov. 1/1: It was so in the manufacturing districts, when every \xe2\x80\x98rattle-box\xe2\x80\x99 became a charnel house, parishes were depopulated [etc.].

2. (Irish) the male genitals.

1966P. Boyle At Night All Cats Are Grey 69: What\xe2\x80\x99s this? You\xe2\x80\x99re minus your rattley box, my good man.

3. see rattle-head n.

rattle can \n (n.)\n

(Ulster) a noisy child.

rattle-cap \n (n.)\n [SE cap, head]

a volatile, unsteady person.
