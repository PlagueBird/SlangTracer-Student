queer v.

1. to quiz or ridicule, to puzzle.

1788Morris et al. \xe2\x80\x98Knowing Joe\xe2\x80\x99 Festival of Anacreon (1810) 51: No boy of my age could talk louder, / Crack a joke, tip the wink, or a droll story tell, / Of my cleverness none were prouder: / So thinks I it\xe2\x80\x99s better not following plow, / To try with these youths to queer low folk.
a.1790C. Dibdin \xe2\x80\x98The Waggoner\xe2\x80\x99 Collection of Songs II 182: Your natty sparks and flashy dames / How I do love to queer, / I runs my rigs, / And patters, and gigs, / And plays a hundred comical games.
1795Sporting Mag. Dec. VII 163/1: A sheepish flat I can queer and bam.
1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue (3rd edn) n.p.: To Queer. To puzzle or confound. I have queered the old full bottom; i.e. I have puzzled the judge.
1799Sporting Mag. Jan. XIII 233/2: For Tom was a wit [...] / And he\xe2\x80\x99d queer the old putt, for his long-winded grace.
1800G. Colman Yngr Heir at Law II ii: Pish, now you be queering a body.
c.1805 \xe2\x80\x98The Rage\xe2\x80\x99 Jovial Songster 19: Be\xe2\x80\x99t to bam, or to hoax, or to queer, or to quis, / Or howe\xe2\x80\x99er in the ton you are flashing.
1811Lex. Balatronicum [as cit. 1796].
1818W. Scott Heart of Mid-Lothian (1883) 272: Come now Jeanie, ye are but queering us.
1821\xe2\x80\x98An Amateur\xe2\x80\x99 Real Life in London I 67: Queering the clergyman at his father\xe2\x80\x99s table.
1826\xe2\x80\x98Nocturnal Sports\xe2\x80\x99 in Universal Songster II 180/2: The beak [...] queered us vith his law-grammar, haxing hus demmed cramp questions ve couldn\xe2\x80\x99t hunderstand.
1832J. Miller Complete Jest Book 262: He had gloriously queered old full-bottom.
1841 \xe2\x80\x98The Four & Ninepenny Hat\xe2\x80\x99 Dublin Comic Songster 102: But of all the wonders of the day, / That queer each sage and flat [...] Is the four and ninepenny hat, sirs.
c.1850Peeping Tom (London) 38 149/1: [In] Young Hellfinches own words, he queer\xe2\x80\x99s old full-bottom and got off.
1856C. Reade It Is Never Too Late to Mend II 245: You may bully him and queer him till all is blue, and he won\xe2\x80\x99t budge.
1876C. Hindley Life and Adventures of a Cheap Jack 111: He had a way of getting out of the dilemma by what he called \xe2\x80\x98queering his opponent\xe2\x80\x99.
1977S. Kernochan Dry Hustle 12: She paused, waiting for my eyes to stop queering.

2. to impose on, to swindle, to cheat; thus queer a flat, to hoodwink a gullible victim.

1781G. Parker View of Society II 128: The man commences the practice of the art of Queering the woman who takes money.
1797M. Robinson Walsingham IV 277: Fine news! \xe2\x80\x93 I\xe2\x80\x99m dished \xe2\x80\x93 done up. The sharps have queered me.
1819\xe2\x80\x98The Young Prig\xe2\x80\x99 in Farmer Musa Pedestris (1896) 83: There no queering fate, sirs .
1823Byron Don Juan canto XI line 147: Who in a row like Tom could lead the van, [...] Who queer a flat? Who (spite of Bow-street\xe2\x80\x99s ban) On the high-toby-spice so flash the muzzle?
c.1830 \xe2\x80\x98Smith\xe2\x80\x99s Frolic\xe2\x80\x99 in Holloway & Black II (1979) 62: Then up a dark alley I went with my whore / Ma\xe2\x80\x99am thought of my bit for to queer me was sure.
c.1835 \xe2\x80\x98Life of a Vagabond\xe2\x80\x99 in Holloway & Black II (1979) 64: Hocussing cocknies, queering flats.
1838W.N. Glascock Land Sharks and Sea Gulls II 4: You\xe2\x80\x99re a good un, Timmins at queering the flats.
1843W.T. Moncrieff Scamps of London II iii: I\xe2\x80\x99ll queer them yet.
1857 in Punch \xe2\x80\x98Dear Bill, This Stone-Jug\xe2\x80\x99 31 Jan. n.p.: In the dayrooms the cuffins we queers at our ease, / And at Darkmans we run the rig just as we please.
1860Hotten Dict. of Modern Sl. etc. (2nd edn) 196: \xe2\x80\x98To queer a flat,\xe2\x80\x99 to puzzle or confound a \xe2\x80\x98gull\xe2\x80\x99 or silly fellow.
1867Swindon Advertiser 11 Nov. 4/1: I taught \xe2\x80\x99em [...] how to slip out of one\xe2\x80\x99s skin, and another slip in [...] And, if copped, to queer the jug by making up a mug.
1878 \xe2\x80\x98\xe2\x80\x99Arry on Himself\xe2\x80\x99 Punch 21 Dec. in P. Marks (2006) 6: But you\xe2\x80\x99ll only queer flats in that fashion, the sharp sort is bound to be fly.
1882Sydney Sl. Dict. (2 edn) 7: To \xe2\x80\x98queer\xe2\x80\x99 a flat, to puzzle or confound a gull.
1890Sporting Times 3 May 3/1: But it was rough on Curtis to queer him at the most critical moment.
1895C. Crowe Aus. Sl. Dict. [as 1882].
1904Marvel 12 Nov. 6: Don\xe2\x80\x99t you queer the biz, where\xe2\x80\x99s the jooils, Foxy?
1934(con. 1920s) J.T. Farrell Young Manhood in Studs Lonigan (1936) 411: I had a job [...] all fixed up a couple of years ago. And that louse queered it thinking he could get it.
1949Monteleone Criminal Sl. (rev. edn) 187: queer a flat To swindle; to cheat; to defraud.

3. to spoil, to put out of order.

a.1790C. Dibdin \xe2\x80\x98The Waggoner\xe2\x80\x99 Collection of Songs II 183: Ball plunges, and paints him all over with mud, / Queers his stockings, and spoils the beau!
1812Vaux Vocab. of the Flash Lang.
1818W. Perry London Guide 142: This mode is not taunting the distress of others: it is nothing more or less, than queering the attempt of a bold beggar to impose upon your softness.
1820Commercial Advertiser (N.Y.) 19 May 2/5: Our readers are not used to such scientific expressions as \xe2\x80\x94 \xe2\x80\x98close-enough-in\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 \xe2\x80\x98giving a rattler on the chest\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 \xe2\x80\x98hit well left and right, and one of his peepers queered\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 \xe2\x80\x98giving a hit which crimsoned well,\xe2\x80\x99 &c. &c.
1824Pierce Egan\xe2\x80\x99s Life in London 7 Nov. 324/3: And dominos chattered, and peepers were queer\xe2\x80\x99d ; / Hard nobbers, and jobbers, and claret galore, Sir.
1830Lytton Paul Clifford I 130: Queer my glims, if that be n\xe2\x80\x99t little Paul!
1846Swell\xe2\x80\x99s Night Guide 64: He [...] split upon him, when he crack\xe2\x80\x99d a crib. / And had him pinch\xe2\x80\x99d; and quodded, too [...] Swore \xe2\x80\x93 he, the deed had stagg\xe2\x80\x99d. So queer\xe2\x80\x99d Bill\xe2\x80\x99s council, and got Billy lagged.

1876C. Hindley Life and Adventures of a Cheap Jack 218: The disease \xe2\x80\x93 consumption \xe2\x80\x93 \xe2\x80\x98was queering him\xe2\x80\x99.
1889Referee 26 May n.p.: Why should not our non-professor\xe2\x80\x99s little game be queered? [F&H].
1890Sporting Times 18 Jan. 1: She unexpectedly queered him by saying\xe2\x80\x94\xe2\x80\x98Well, at any rate, the flea you complained of this morning was caught off me\xe2\x80\x99.
1895S.F. Chron. 6 June 11/5: De lampers stood \xe2\x80\x99round till dey got wise to de joint, an\xe2\x80\x99 dey didn\xe2\x80\x99t do a t\xe2\x80\x99ing but queer me marks.
1900G.R. Sims In London\xe2\x80\x99s Heart 84: If he was to come in while we was on the premises it might queer the thing altogether.
1910Truth (Perth) 1 Oct. 4/7: If he\xe2\x80\x99s badly \xe2\x80\x98narked\xe2\x80\x99 at that, / You may know, / That the \xe2\x80\x98bally koshermen / Queered the show\xe2\x80\x99 .
1917F. Packard Adventures of Jimmie Dale (1918) 17: He could always \xe2\x80\x98queer\xe2\x80\x99 a game in some specious manner, if he were pushed too far.
1920F.S. Fitzgerald This Side of Paradise in Bodley Head Scott Fitzgerald III (1960) 241: Food is what queered the party.
1930R. Whitfield Green Ice (1988) 41: That queered the suicide gag.
1940R. Chandler Farewell, My Lovely (1949) 238: That queered it.
1952(con. 1948) G. Mandel Flee the Angry Strangers 45: I don\xe2\x80\x99t want no Dills Hotel whore queerin the joint fer all the respec\xe2\x80\x99bul faces.

1974G.F. Newman You Flash Bastard 34: An empty shop two doors along. If they queer the jeweller\xe2\x80\x99s security system, then go in through the basement.
1999(con. 1921) R. Doyle A Star Called Henry (2000) 313: She\xe2\x80\x99s queering things for the rest of us.
2004 \xe2\x80\x98Animated Dominoes, Dice\xe2\x80\x99 at Old and Sold Any professional crap shooter\xe2\x80\x99s game was queered if the quicksilver expanded and fell out [of crooked dice].
2023(con. 1962) J. Ellroy Enchanters 31: You chose the wrong one, and it queered your relationship.

4. to act in an odd manner.

1826R. Morley \xe2\x80\x98Flashey Joe\xe2\x80\x99 in Farmer Musa Pedestris (1896) 96: Vhy, then you swore you would he kind / But you have queer\xe2\x80\x99d so much of late.
1978R. Price Ladies\xe2\x80\x99 Man (1985) 4: My stomach queered and I couldn\xe2\x80\x99t do another sit-up.

5. (UK und.) to rob.

1840Flash Mirror 6: Queering of a Duff Shop. \xe2\x80\x94 Going into an eating house, calling for a go of soup, prigging the knives and forks, pocketing the saltcellars [and] seizing a roll of duff, and paddling off scot-free.

6. (UK Und.) to pose as.

1847G.M.W. Reynolds Mysteries of London III 66/1: His jomen [sic] Mutton-Face Sal, with her moll-sack queering a raclan, stalled.

7. of a person, to spoil the reputation of, to spoil someone\xe2\x80\x99s efforts or opportunities.

1861Melbourne Punch \xe2\x80\x98The Lay of the Lags\xe2\x80\x99 14 Mar. 1/1: Then when all the bobbies blewed are, / We can buz and rim a billy, / None to pall the trick and queer us, / Sending us to stun on skilly.
1885 \xe2\x80\x98\xe2\x80\x99Arry [...] at the Grosvenor Gallery\xe2\x80\x99 Punch 10 Jan. 24/2: Give yer my davy it queers / A snide \xe2\x80\x99un to trot round these rooms.
1893S. Crane Maggie, a Girl of the Streets (2001) 47: Well, look-a-here, dis t\xe2\x80\x99ing queers us! See? We\xe2\x80\x99re queered!
1900Flynt & Walton Powers That Prey 21: See all the trouble he\xe2\x80\x99s givin\xe2\x80\x99 us. He\xe2\x80\x99ll queer the whole of us if we don\xe2\x80\x99t get him.
1910O. Johnson Varmint 88: I\xe2\x80\x99m disgraced [...] It\xe2\x80\x99s all over \xe2\x80\x93 all over. I\xe2\x80\x99m queered \xe2\x80\x93 queered forever!
1914S. Lewis Our Mr Wrenn (1936) 231: Good Lord, suppose Istra \xe2\x80\x98queered\xe2\x80\x99 him at Mrs. Arty\xe2\x80\x99s!
1925Dos Passos Manhattan Transfer 191: If there\xe2\x80\x99s one thing that\xe2\x80\x99ll queer you in this town it\xe2\x80\x99s this labor stuff.
1930E. Glyn Flirt and Flapper 81: Flapper: He had returned to see me \xe2\x80\x94 but Lollie\xe2\x80\x99s queered him again \xe2\x80\x94 and he\xe2\x80\x99s going to Mexico.
1937W.M. Raine Cool Customer 285: He doesn\xe2\x80\x99t know enough about my game to queer it.
1940D. Maurer Big Con 147: You can [...] see that no one gets to him to queer the deal.
1953F. Paley Rumble on the Docks (1955) 189: Imagine this little bitch trying to queer her deal with Brindo!
1965S. Yurick Warriors (1966) 21: Look at that; he queering you with a look.
1970E. Thompson Garden of Sand (1981) 431: She tried to talk to Jacky with her eyes, warning him not to queer the setup.
2002D.H. Sterry Chicken (2003) 32: It\xe2\x80\x99s my theory that she really wants to be with a woman, but I\xe2\x80\x99m not about to tell her that. Don\xe2\x80\x99t want to queer a good thing.

8. (US) to cause trouble for.

1913\xe2\x80\x9314Van Loan \xe2\x80\x98The Revenge of Kid Morales\xe2\x80\x99 in Taking the Count 284: Smith never lets up on a fellow that dogs it [...] He\xe2\x80\x99ll queer you and chase you out of the business.

9. to sexually abuse.

1958W. Talsman Gaudy Image (1966) 40: \xe2\x80\x98I thought so! [...] Been here a week and queered every guy on the place.\xe2\x80\x99 She swung her broom madly.

10. to highlight the homosexual aspects of a given form of creativity, e.g. painting.

2017Guardian Online 6 Feb. At the beginning [Hockney\xe2\x80\x99s] art was a queering of British abstraction [...] a ticker tape of toilet graffiti, cottaging and sex in the shower.

\n In derivatives\n

queerer \n (n.)\n

(prizefighting) a blow that tells heavily on its recipient.

1826Pierce Egan\xe2\x80\x99s Life in London 26 Feb. 453/1: \xe2\x80\x98That is a queerer he has given your man \xe2\x80\x94 the jaw is almost knocked off him\xe2\x80\x99.
