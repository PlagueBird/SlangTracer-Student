monkey adj.

1. (US) a general derog., esp. in racist contexts.

1842Whip & Satirist of NY & Brooklyn (NY) 18 June n.p.: These monkey Frenchmen.
1904\xe2\x80\x98O. Henry\xe2\x80\x99 Cabbages & Kings 201: [of Central America] \xe2\x80\x98We struck the monkey coast one afternoon [...] The monkeys were loading her up with big barges\xe2\x80\x99.
1926E. Walrond Tropic Death (1972) 64: Hey, Porto Rico, snap into it! Dis ain\xe2\x80\x99t no time to get foolin\xe2\x80\x99 wit\xe2\x80\x99 no monkey jane.
1929C. McKay Banjo 125: \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99ll tell you a real man story, pardner,\xe2\x80\x99 said Banjo, \xe2\x80\x98that ain\xe2\x80\x99t no monkey-coon affair.\xe2\x80\x99.
1939J. Cary Mister Johnson (1952) 123: I know the law as well as you do, Mr. Monkeybrand.
1952B. Appel Plunder (2005) 261: The Red bastids [...] that\xe2\x80\x99s what they want now \xe2\x80\x93 a goddam monkey revolution. [Ibid.] 303: A light voice questioned him in Tagalog. Monkey talk, he thought.
1968Champion Jack Dupree & Mickey Baker \xe2\x80\x98I\xe2\x80\x99m Tired of Moanin\xe2\x80\x99\xe2\x80\x99 Well I\xe2\x80\x99m leaving heeeee, swear I can\xe2\x80\x99t take you / \xe2\x80\x99Cause there\xe2\x80\x99s nothing now baby, a monkey woman like you can do.
1991Cooper & Wright New Jack City [film script] He\xe2\x80\x99s kicking his monkey ass.
1994N. McCall Makes Me Wanna Holler (1995) 303: Should I straighten his monkey ass here and now.

2. difficult, troublesome.

1977(con. 1950s) Nicholson & Smith Spend, Spend, Spend (1978) 55: Matt\xe2\x80\x99s mother would untidy it [...] Then If I told her off she\xe2\x80\x99d get really monkey about it.
1999Guardian Guide 22\xe2\x80\x9328 May 6: You and your monkey friends are ruining it for everybody else.

3. corrupt, illicit.

1974G.F. Newman You Flash Bastard 159: Sneed had another bent account in Pall Mall which no-one knew about. He had opened it with forged references from two monkey addresses, and all the transactions had been by cash.
