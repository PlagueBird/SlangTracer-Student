stiff adj.

[lit. and fig. uses of SE stiff, rigid]

1. in lit. senses.

(a) (orig. US) drunk; esp. very drunk and passed out cold; thus ext. stiff as a board; note earlier sense of stiff drinker n., a hard drinker in cit. 1654.

[1654Witts Recreations \xe2\x80\x98Fancies and Fantasticks\xe2\x80\x99 No. 115: From Court we invite / Lord, Lady, and Knight, [...] And all our stiffe drinkers, / Smiths, Porters, and Tinkers, / And the Beggars shall give ye room].
