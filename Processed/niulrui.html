squeeze v.

1. to bring in trouble, to cause difficulties for.

1804Revolutionary Plutarch III 232: The snuff-box for which I am now pinched, interrupted my career to the consulate for life, in the same manner as a gold bracelet squeezed me in 1796, and prevented me from being a Director .
1822S.A. Mathews Willshire Squeeze 31: I\xe2\x80\x99ll bet ten joes, before he goes, / I squeeze the Yankey dry.
1836Comic Almanack July 60: I wish you were squeez\xe2\x80\x99d, \xe2\x80\x93 and that\xe2\x80\x99s flat, \xe2\x80\x93 / For ill-using a \xe2\x80\x98Maiden forlorn\xe2\x80\x99.
1895H.B. Marriott Watson in New Rev. July, 2: For it was his aim to stand in security somewhere half-way \xe2\x80\x99twixt us fellows and the Law, and squeeze the both; and but that he had the lives of scores upon his tongue, and was very useful withal at a pinch, both to us on the lay and to the traps, he would have been hanged or pistolled for his pains long since [F&H].
1910Bulletin (Sydney) 22 Dec. 16/1: I never seen no squatter goin\xe2\x80\x99 a-beggin\xe2\x80\x99 for his bread \xe2\x80\x93 / But they squeezed Bill Brown when Bill was broke, and very nearly dead.

2. (orig. US) to pressurize, to blackmail; thus squeezability, a potential to suffer extortion (see cite 1868).

1847Era 28 Mar. 10/1: Money had far greater attraction to Ginger than gratitude or friendship; and he held a colloquy with himself as to the best method of \xe2\x80\x98squeezing\xe2\x80\x99 his friend.
1856London Eve. Standard 20 Mar. 2/5: [headline] Weak and Squeezable Whig-Radical Ministry.
1868Sportsman 13 May 2/1: Notes on News [...] [D]efrauding young people [...] according to the squeezeability of the victims.
1879Whitecross and the Bench 188: It did not take long to see that with such information in their possession, the young Guardsman was to be \xe2\x80\x98squeezed\xe2\x80\x99 to a pretty tune.
1884North-Eastern Dly Gaz. 13 Sept. 3/2: [P]retending I was struggling to get an honest living, and squeezing her for a bit of money.
1886Nat. Police Gaz. (NY) 3 July 14/2: Von der Ahe is the only sufferer bv the deal, and if it is not simply \xe2\x80\x98squeezing\xe2\x80\x99 a cool $200 out of the Dutchman, then we don\xe2\x80\x99t know what a \xe2\x80\x98squeeze\xe2\x80\x99 is.
1900Flynt & Walton Powers That Prey 144: He\xe2\x80\x99s too damned crooked. He\xe2\x80\x99s squeezed us blokes right an\xe2\x80\x99 left, an\xe2\x80\x99 put the dough in his own pocket.
1915Truth (Wellington) 22 May 7/5: Winnie took proceedings to the Magistrate\xe2\x80\x99s Court with the idea of having [him] located and squeezed for regular contributions.
1911G. Bronson-Howard Enemy to Society 39: They\xe2\x80\x99re entitled to that and no more\xe2\x80\x94not to all they can squeeze out of the public.
1926Wood & Goddard Dict. Amer. Sl.
1933N. Algren \xe2\x80\x98So Help Me\xe2\x80\x99 in Texas Stories (1995) 26: I figger to myself he musta squeezed the Jew.
1941V. Davis Phenomena in Crime 253: Squeezing. Demanding hush money.
1951M. Spillane Long Wait (1954) 110: I could go back and ask Eddie Packman what the guy did to earn a grand and maybe squeeze him a little to make him talk.
1960C. Cooper Jr Scene (1996) 94: When it comes time to squeeze Ace, he\xe2\x80\x99ll be one more tack in The Man\xe2\x80\x99s back pocket.
c.1973J. Thompson \xe2\x80\x98Sunrise at Midnight\xe2\x80\x99 in Fireworks (1988) 167: You think I\xe2\x80\x99m still squeezing \xe2\x80\x99em? [...] No siree, pal! I never tried for another touch.
1980T. Lewis GBH 90: \xe2\x80\x98[I]f he was squeezing for the Sheps and we went down\xe2\x80\x99 [etc].
1981J. Ellroy Brown\xe2\x80\x99s Requiem 214: He popped Fat Dog for the bombing, but let him slide, because he wanted to squeeze Solly.
1990(con. early 1950s) J. Ellroy L.A. Confidential 142: He could level with Timmy and Billy, shake them down, squeeze their contacts.
1999J. Ridley Love Is a Racket 31: Why you always got to put the bite on me, Amber? [...] city full of souls, and you always got to try to squeeze me.
2005J. Stahl I, Fatty 134: He\xe2\x80\x99s going to squeeze you, Roscoe [...] guilt you into payin\xe2\x80\x99 up big.

3. (US und.) to pay a bribe.

1931Dly News (NY) 3 Mar. 6/2: Gang Slang. Squeeze: graft. \xe2\x80\x98Squeeze the captain and you\xe2\x80\x99re okay in the district\xe2\x80\x99.

4. (orig. US) to inform, to pass on information.

1947M.H. Boulware Jive and Sl. n.p.: Squeeze me ... Tell me.

5. (US teen) to make advances to a friend\xe2\x80\x99s partner.

1981Detroit Free Press (MI) 6 July 17/1: squeeze (are you trying to squeeze?) \xe2\x80\x94 talk to someone else\xe2\x80\x99s girlfriend or boyfriend.

6. (UK black/gang) to fire a gun, to shoot.

2018-20T. Thorne (ed.) \xe2\x80\x98Drill Slang Glossary\xe2\x80\x99 at Forensic Linguistic Databank Squeeze - fire (a gun), shoot (bullets).

\n In compounds\n

squeeze-play \n (n.)\n [play n. (2) + pun on baseball jargon squeeze play, \xe2\x80\x98a tactic whereby the batter bunts so that a runner at third base can attempt to reach home safely and score\xe2\x80\x99 (OED)]

1. (US) the application of lit. or fig. force or pressure to get what one wants.

1916Indep. (NY) 5 June 410: [heading] The \xe2\x80\x98squeeze-play\xe2\x80\x99 at the capital [OED].
1932Sun (Baltimore) 14 Sept. 10/1: Most employers of relatively small numbers of workers, they appear to have been caught in an economic \xe2\x80\x98squeeze play\xe2\x80\x99 in which \xe2\x80\x98contract\xe2\x80\x99 shops [...] are played against each other in pursuit of the lowest bids [OED].
1944D. Wecter When Johnny Comes Marching Home 557: You perhaps mentioned the fact that Hitler was putting the squeeze play on Hindenburg a few years later.
1958R. Prather Scrambled Yeggs 146: That made him a soft touch when Joe Brooks \xe2\x80\x93 or Joey Maddern \xe2\x80\x93 started his little squeeze play.
1976Indep. (Long Beach, CA) 19 Aug. 31/2: City officials were caught by surprise [...] to say this was a \xe2\x80\x98squeeze play\xe2\x80\x99 is putting it mildly.
2005B. Coleman Rakim Told Me 66: \xe2\x80\x98I guess back in the day they would change the single up and remix it for the album so you'd buy both. It was a squeeze play\xe2\x80\x99.

2. pickpocketing.

1947C.S. Montanye \xe2\x80\x98Frozen Stiff\xe2\x80\x99 in Popular Detective Mar. Hagen picked him up for working a squeeze play on a cloak-and-suiter from out of town. Tommy had the visitor\xe2\x80\x99s leather, full of green, in a hip pocket, along with his flash.

squeeze me! [pron.]

(US campus) excuse me!

1996Eble Sl. and Sociability 39: Manipulating sounds for fun is consistent with the flippant, venturesome spirit of much slang use. Hence the polite apology Excuse me is playfully pronounced Screws me, Squeeze me, or Exsqueeze me.
