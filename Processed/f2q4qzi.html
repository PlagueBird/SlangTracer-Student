bumper n.3

[ext. of bum n.1 (1) + echoic of the buttocks hitting a hard surface]

1. (US) a trunk.

1887Daily Trib. (Bismarck, ND) 23 Oct. 4/1: A trunk is a \xe2\x80\x98bumper\xe2\x80\x99.

2. (US/W.I.) the buttocks [? play on keister n. (1)/keister n. (5)].

1995Francis-Jackson Official Dancehall Dict. 7: Bumper the behind: u. woman, me love yu bumper/ I love your rear end.
2013K. Koke \xe2\x80\x98Could Have Been\xe2\x80\x99 Pretty face, small waist, big bump-per. / Tight pum, firm boobs in her jump-per.
2015J. Spades \xe2\x80\x98Twerk\xe2\x80\x99 Gyal dem shakin their bumpers / Twerk, twerk, twerk, twerk, twerk, twerk, turn up.

3. usu. in pl., the female breasts; occas. buttocks.

c.1943\xe2\x80\x98Bill O. Lading\xe2\x80\x99 You Chirped a Chinful!! n.p.: Bumpers: Upper front or lower back of a girl.
1948(con. 1944) N. Mailer Naked and Dead 485: What bumpers on her.
1960(con. 1950-1960) R.A. Freeman Dict. Inmate Sl. (Walla Walla, WA) 20: Bumpers \xe2\x80\x93 a woman\xe2\x80\x99s breastworks.
1966C. Wood Fill the Stage With Happy Hours (1967) Act IV: Couple of sequins on her bumpers.
1970\xe2\x80\x98Red\xe2\x80\x99 Rudensky Gonif 102: Babes with big Bumpers Wanted. For Shipboard Duty.
1977A. Bleasdale Who\xe2\x80\x99s Been Sleeping in my Bed 48: Those bumpers of hers they\xe2\x80\x99re like the tip ends of torpedoes.
1985\xe2\x80\x98Jennifer Blowdryer\xe2\x80\x99 Modern English 77: tits (n): Bumpers.
1988J. McDonald Dict. of Obscenity etc.
1990S. Morgan Homeboy 15: Joe rebounded two feet off the fortyfour triple-D bionic bumpers attached to Bermuda Schwartze.
2001A. Sillitoe Birthday 92: But I still like nudging into your front bumpers.
2005Jade LB Keisha the Sket (2021) 25: [of the buttocks] I climbed on top of his buff body, wit my bumpa in da air and ma lips on his.

4. (US) the vagina.

1974G.V. Higgins Cogan\xe2\x80\x99s Trade (1975) 44: I should\xe2\x80\x99ve told her, go out and throw the bumper around.

5. (US prison) in fig. use (see car n. (2)) the status of working for qualification as a gang member, thus phr. on the bumper.

2019D. bumper to bumper \n (adv.)\n

(US lesbian) of lesbians, vagina to vagina, said of dancing or sexual intercourse.

1972B. Rodgers Queens\xe2\x80\x99 Vernacular 38: bumper-to-bumper 1. tribadism, i.e. rubbing vulvas in a face-to face position \xe2\x80\x98Not all gay women do it bumper-to-bumper; some prefer sixty-nining instead\xe2\x80\x99. 2. close, intimate dancing or standing close together, vagina to vagina.
