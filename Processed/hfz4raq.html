chop v.1

also chop up
[SE chop, to thrust or move with force or suddenness]

1. (UK Und.) to do something quickly.

1584Three Ladies of London II: Will ye buy any broome. Who bargen or chop with conscience, what will no customer come?
1600Dekker Shoemakers\xe2\x80\x99 Holiday IV iv: They in the meane time shall chop vp the matter at the Sauoy.
1637R. Brome Eng. Moor I i: l heard the bravest noise / Of laughter at a wicked accident / Of Marriage, that was chopt up this Morning.
1741Witchcraft of Love 29: What an if her Father should chop up the Wedding to Day, for he likes me woundily.
1785, 1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue n.p.: To chop, in the canting sense, means making dispatch, or hurrying over any business.
1851R.S. Surtees Young Tom Hall (1926) 32: Making up to this man when told he was a \xe2\x80\x98catch\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 chopping over to that when advised he was \xe2\x80\x98better\xe2\x80\x99.

2. (UK Und.) to speak, esp. hurriedly (cf. chop (up) the whiners under whiners n.).

c.1698B.E. Dict. Canting Crew n.p.: To chop up Prayers, to huddle them up, or slubber them over.
1725New Canting Dict. n.p.: To chop is also used to make Dispatch, to slubber over in Post-haste.
1922\xe2\x80\x98J.H. Ross\xe2\x80\x99 Mint (1955) 156: The two warriors sat beside the stove [...] to chop tales of old wild service.

3. (US black) to drive an automobile.

2001UGK \xe2\x80\x98Choppin\xe2\x80\x99 Blades\xe2\x80\x99 I made this for the niggas tryin\xe2\x80\x99 ta chop in they cars.

\n In phrases\n

chop it up \n (v.)\n (also chop mouth)

(US) to discuss, to talk about.

1950Goldin et al. DAUL 44/1: Chop it up. To talk; to exchange underworld yarn.
1953R. Mais Hills were Joyful Together (1966) 34: Yuh better gwan \xe2\x80\x99bout yuh business, bwoy, Ah don\xe2\x80\x99t feel to chop mout\xe2\x80\x99 wid yuh today.
1956H. Gold Man Who Was Not With It (1965) 192: Don\xe2\x80\x99t make me chop it up tonight.
1962\xe2\x80\x938 in B. Jackson In the Life (1972) 252: I don\xe2\x80\x99t enjoy talking to people that I don\xe2\x80\x99t believe are my peers. But I do enjoy just chopping it up, kicking it around, and seeing what other people seem to think.
2010A. Steinberg Running the Books 243: He came to the library to \xe2\x80\x98chop it up,\xe2\x80\x99 to \xe2\x80\x98converse and conversate\xe2\x80\x99.
2019N. Walker Cherry 164: The battalion sergeant major was out there chopping it up with [Specialist] Grace, and he wanted all of us to hear him talking.
2021T. Swerdlow Straight Dope [ebook] [M]e and her would chop it up about books and writers.
