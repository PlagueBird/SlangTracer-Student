twist n.1

1. the penis [a \xe2\x80\x98twist\xe2\x80\x99 of flesh].

1672J. Phillips Maronides (1678) VI 19: Though they came out of Jove\xe2\x80\x99s own Twist, / Or from a Goddess engine pist.

2. a drink of tea and coffee mixed together.

c.1698B.E. Dict. Canting Crew n.p.: Twist half Tea, half Coffee.
1725New Canting Dict. [as cit. c.1698].
1737, 1759, 1760, 1776Bailey Universal Etym. Eng. Dict. [as cit. c.1698].
1785, 1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1823Egan Grose\xe2\x80\x99s Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.

3. any mixed alcoholic drink, typically brandy and eggs or brandy, beer and eggs, brandy and gin or a gin twist, gin and hot water.

1685G. Meriton In Praise of York-shire Ale 3: Twist, Old Pharoh, and Old Hoc.
1725New Canting Dict. n.p.: twist [...] Brandy and Eggs mixed. Hot-Pot.
1737, 1759, 1760, 1776Bailey Universal Etym. Eng. Dict. [as cit. 1725].
1785, 1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue n.p.: twist [...] brandy, beer, and eggs.
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1814J. Wetherell Adventures of John Wetherell (1954) 26 Jan. 206: We rested a while took a draw of the pipe and we took another twist of eau de vie.
1823Egan Grose\xe2\x80\x99s Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.
1850Thackeray Pendennis II 1: When he went to the Back Kitchen that night [...] the gin twist and devilled turkey had no charms for him.
1864, 1867, 1870Hotten Sl. Dict.
1900Eve. Teleg. (Dundee) 1 Sept. 3/6: The language of the London East-end pub [...] \xe2\x80\x98Twist\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x94 Brandy and gin.

4. the hangman\xe2\x80\x99s noose.

c.1800 \xe2\x80\x98The Blue Lion\xe2\x80\x99 in Holloway & Black I (1975) 32: Upon the scaffold he doth come / The twist they then do tie on.

5. the arm lock forced on a person who is being arrested.

1960C. MacInnes Mr Love and Justice (1964) 162: The tap might come upon his shoulder (or, to modernize the metaphor, the twist might come upon his biceps).

6. (Irish) a quarrel, an argument; thus phrs. in good twist, on good terms; in bad twist, on bad terms; in twist, in agreement.

1943(con. 1850s) G.A. Little Malachi Horan Remembers 80: In a short while they got out of twist.
1943G.A. Little Malachi Horan Remembers 70: She tried every turn with him; but him and her seemed never in twist.
1997Share Slanguage.

7. cheating, dishonesty, treachery ; thus at the twist, double-crossing; on the twist, thieving.

1895Western Kansas World (Wakeeney, KS) 20 July 6/2: \xe2\x80\x98They was aimin\xe2\x80\x99 to git away with all them nocturnes [...] an\xe2\x80\x99 not say nothin\xe2\x80\x99 [...] but they can come no twist like that an\xe2\x80\x99 me ridin\xe2\x80\x98 herd. None whatever!\xe2\x80\x99.
1903A.H. Lewis Boss 362: That twist will work through all right.
1920Bedford & Sullivan [perf. Marie Lloyd] One of the ruins Cromwell knocked about a bit The pot-man's name is Oliver and he\xe2\x80\x99s always on the twist.
1937J. Curtis You\xe2\x80\x99re in the Racket, Too 66: Awful twist, really, you know. I must say your father\xe2\x80\x99s an accomplished old scoundrel.
1943\xe2\x80\x98Myles na gCopaleen\xe2\x80\x99 Faustus Kelly in \xe2\x80\x98Flann O\xe2\x80\x99Brien\xe2\x80\x99 Stories & Plays (1973) 126: Ten to one you\xe2\x80\x99re off to Dublin to work some election twist for Kelly.
1958M. Allingham Hide My Eyes (1960) 66: The whole blessed place seems to be on the twist these days.

8. (Aus.) a professional criminal [abbr. twister n. (4) or ? not being straight adj.1 (3)].

1941Baker Popular Dict. Aus. Sl.
1945Baker Aus. Lang.
1967 \xe2\x80\x98Whisper All Aussie Dict.\xe2\x80\x99 in Kings Cross Whisper (Sydney) xli 4/5: twist: Same as key man.
1990Tupper & Wortley Aus. Prison Sl. Gloss. Twist. An habitual criminal.

9. (Aus Und.) an indeterminate sentence.

1931Sun (Sydney) 17 Aug. 7/6: At the police station [...] Walker said, \xe2\x80\x98I suppose I\xe2\x80\x99ll get the \xe2\x80\x98twist\xe2\x80\x99 (indeterminate sentence) for this.\xe2\x80\x99 .

10. (drugs) a marijuana cigarette [it is twisted into shape].

1935A.J. Pollock Und. Speaks n.p.: Twist, sufficient marihuana for one or two cigarettes. Twist scranner, a person who picks up the ends or butts of smoked marihuana cigarettes and makes new cigarettes from them.
1936D. Maurer \xe2\x80\x98Argot of the Und. Narcotic Addict\xe2\x80\x99 Pt 1 in AS XI:2 127/1: twist. A marajuana [sic] cigarette.
1949Monteleone Criminal Sl. (rev. edn).
1955E. Hunter Blackboard Jungle 196: You know what a twist is?
1960D. Lytton Goddam White Man 71: You get your own twist of the stuff [dagga] and what I give you on top.
1971E.E. Landy Underground Dict. (1972).
2001ONDCP Street Terms 22: Twist \xe2\x80\x94 Marijuana cigarette.

11. (Aus.) a key.

1946Singleton Argus (NSW) 4/2: Other fancy underworld terms for [a key] are \xe2\x80\x98the twirl,\xe2\x80\x99 and \xe2\x80\x98the gate,\xe2\x80\x99 and \xe2\x80\x98the twist\xe2\x80\x99.
1983R. Aven-Bray Ridgey-Didge Oz Jack Lang 49: Twist Key.

12. (Aus. Und.) an indeterminate prison sentence.

1950S.J. Baker in Sun. Herald (Sydney) 8 June 9/3: A crook who earns a \xe2\x80\x98Kathleen Mavourneen\xe2\x80\x99 (that is, \xe2\x80\x98It may be for years and it may be forever . . .\xe2\x80\x99), \xe2\x80\x98key,\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98twist,\xe2\x80\x99 or \xe2\x80\x98The Act\xe2\x80\x99 has been declared an habitual criminal with an indeterminant sentence.

13. (Aus./US) a dishonest, untrustworthy person.

1953\xe2\x80\x98John Eagle\xe2\x80\x99 Hoodlums (2021) 127: The M. C. was listing [...] what passed for celebrities in the El Conejo. \xe2\x80\x98Judge So and So,\xe2\x80\x99 and some goony wizened twist [...] would get up to take a bow.
1955N. Pulliam I Travelled a Lonely Land (1957) 241/1: twist \xe2\x80\x93 [...] a cheater.

14. (US Und.) a sexually eccentric individual, often a prostiute\xe2\x80\x99s client; also attrib.

1961T.I. Rubin In the Life 117: Maybe a queer John or a twist or something.
1963 in T.I. Rubin Sweet Daddy 11: I\xe2\x80\x99ve seen twists in my time. Dumpers, what not. [...] there\xe2\x80\x99s plenty bread in the twist business.
2005R. Cea No Lights, No Sirens 241: How\xe2\x80\x99s it feel, you motherfucking rapist, you twist, you scumbag?
2021J. Ellroy Widespread Panic 305: That sick twist Jimmy Dean was all hopped up to play Chessman.

15. (Irish) a turn.

1825T.C. Croker Fairy Legends and Traditions of the South of Ireland (1862) 205: I can have another twist at it.
1972(con. 1930s\xe2\x80\x9340s) N. Conway Bloods 65: One mixed-up semi-soldier from civvie-land, doing his first twist of the two-on and four-off, successfully challenged the visiting Orderly Officer.
1990R. Doyle Snapper 115: Whose twist is it? said Bertie.

16. (drugs) a small bag of heroin secured with a twist tie.

2001ONDCP Street Terms 22: Twists \xe2\x80\x94 Small plastic bags of heroin secured with a twist tie.

\n In phrases\n

on a twist \n (phr.)\n

(US) on a (drunken) spree.

1903Nebraska State Jrnl (Lincoln, NE) 14 June 9/6: The dip [...] hadn\xe2\x80\x99t been on a twist for four months.
take a twist out of \n (v.)\n

(Aus. Und.) to abuse verbally, to swear at.

1932Sun. Mail (Brisbane) 13 Nov. 20/7: \xe2\x80\x98Joe was buckled last night. He was all keyed up with angie and tried to take a twist out of a demon, he dug his heels in and it took three of them to lumber him.\xe2\x80\x99 [...] Joe was under the influence of cocaine. He used insulting language to a detective, and resisted so violently when placed under arrest that it took the detective and two other officers to remove him to the watch house.
