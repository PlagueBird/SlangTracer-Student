rig n.2

[? dial.]

1. (also rigg, rigging) a dodge, a confidence trick, thus throw rigging, to perpetrate such a trick.

1725New Canting Dict. n.p.: rig Game, Diversion, Ridicule.
1737, 1759, 1760, 1776Bailey Universal Etym. Eng. Dict. [as cit. 1725].
1753J. Poulter Discoveries (1774) 18: We went out at Night on our old Rigg.
1775 \xe2\x80\x98Potato Man\xe2\x80\x99 in Farmer Musa Pedestris (1896) ) 55: I\xe2\x80\x99m up to all your knowing rigs.
1781G. Parker View of Society II 39: Men not having horses, who are on the Foot-pad Rig. [Ibid.] 55: When you have stood this rig, he begins to work you upon another.
1794Sporting Mag. Oct. V 5/2: hairb: Was it high fun? rump: Rare rig!
1797\xe2\x80\x98T.B. Junr.\xe2\x80\x99 Pettyfogger Dramatized I i: What\xe2\x80\x99s the rigg now? [Ibid.] 109: Rigg. Mischief or Roguery.
c.1800 \xe2\x80\x98Drunk in the Night\xe2\x80\x99 No. 26 Papers of Francis Place (1819) n.p.: The scouts all came up being flash to the rig.
1809G. Andrewes Dict. Sl. and Cant.
1814I. Pocock John of Paris II i: I see how it is, you\xe2\x80\x99re a wag, Mr. John\xe2\x80\x99s man, you\xe2\x80\x99re a queer one,\xe2\x80\x94you wanted to frighten me, but it won\xe2\x80\x99t do; I\xe2\x80\x99m up to your rig.
1829Vidocq Memoirs (trans. W. McGinn) II 160: Where there is enough for two, there is enough for four: thus we settled it, and they were to be in the rig with us.
1836Marryat Japhet 8: Sometimes I carry on my rigs a little too far.
c.1840 \xe2\x80\x98Mr. Ferguson and Queen Victoria\xe2\x80\x99 in C. Hindley Curiosities of Street Lit. (1871) 63: The next was Mr. Broomstick / With him she play\xe2\x80\x99d a rig.
1853Life in Boston & N.Y. (Boston, MA) 8 Oct. n.p.: I didn\xe2\x80\x99t suppose a man of your cloth would throw rigging. Rather low, that.
1858A. Mayhew Paved with Gold 143: \xe2\x80\x98That\xe2\x80\x99s a queer way of doing business.\xe2\x80\x99 [...] \xe2\x80\x98Wait till Whit-Monday and you\xe2\x80\x99ll see worse rigs than them.\xe2\x80\x99.
1868 \xe2\x80\x98Rocky Road to Dublin\xe2\x80\x99 in My Young Wife and I Songster 52: Down among the pigs, / I played some funny rigs.
1872G.P. Burnham Memoirs of the US Secret Service 97: During the rebellion, he run a new rig.
1880Henley & Stevenson Deacon Brodie I tab.I vii: brodie: The old move, I presume? the double set of dice? smith: That\xe2\x80\x99s the rig, Deakin. What you drop on the square you pick up again on the cross.
1899\xe2\x80\x931900C.L. Cullen Tales of the Ex-Tanks 44: I feared they might tumble to the rig and get next to me.
1902J. Masefield \xe2\x80\x98Burial-Party\xe2\x80\x99 in Salt-Water Ballads 11: It\xe2\x80\x99s a rummy rig of a guffy\xe2\x80\x99s yarn.
1915\xe2\x80\x98Ian Hay\xe2\x80\x99 First Hundred Thousand (1918) 277: I suppose there\xe2\x80\x99s some low-down political rig at the back of it all.
1955\xe2\x80\x98Blackie\xe2\x80\x99 Audett Rap Sheet 79: Real tough boys that would as soon let you have it as look at you, unless they knowed you was in on the rig.
1991(con. 1920s) O.D. Brooks Legs 25: A loaf of punk you get from putting the rigging on [...] a baker.
1997\xe2\x80\x932000Da Bomb 24: Rig: A trick or cunning deception.

2. ridicule, mockery.

1725New Canting Dict.
1760B. Weatherby Great News from Hell 14: I began my old Rig, and gave them such a Paterero as brought the Devil himself to the Gate.
1795A Puff at the Guinea Pigs n.p.: To call a man like me a hog! \xe2\x80\x93 a very pretty rig, sir!
1819P. Egan Key to the Picture of the Fancy going to a Fight 19: [T]he rig is too strong for his delicate nerves to encounter.
1820W. Combe Doctor Syntax, Consolation (1868) 160/1: The rats, it seems, had play\xe2\x80\x99d the rig / In tearing up the Doctor\xe2\x80\x99s wig. [Ibid.] 198/1: He ne\xe2\x80\x99er saw the Sportsmen\xe2\x80\x99s tricks, / Who, slyly, had contriv\xe2\x80\x99d to fix / A Fox\xe2\x80\x99s brush, by way of rig, / To dangle from the Doctor\xe2\x80\x99s wig.
1840T. Haliburton Letter-bag of the Great Western (1873) 44: To-day, when I fell on the broad of my back, they began running their rig as usual, saying, \xe2\x80\x98Pull down your smockfrock, John Skinner, or you\xe2\x80\x99ll show your legs\xe2\x80\x99.
1846D. Corcoran Picking from N.O. Picayune 84: \xe2\x80\x98Folks call me Hopand Go Constant, by way of a rig\xe2\x80\x99 [...] said the odd legged man.

3. a prank or game.

c.1775H. Carey Honest Yorkshire-Man 18: O Bartledom [i.e. Bartholemew] Fair / [...] / Such Pork, Such Pig, / Such Game, Such Rig.
1831Satirist (London) 14 Aug. 151/1: \xe2\x80\x98The young Falkners,\xe2\x80\x99 as a packet of little gentlemen [...] are called, have been kicking up some strange rigs among the minor divinities [...] ladies as easy of access here [i.e. in Brighton] as the fair dealers in the purlieus.
1845J. Lindridge Sixteen-String Jack 206: So now I\xe2\x80\x99m on the boozing rig.
1850Life in Boston & N.Y. (Boston, MA) 10 Aug. n.p.: She runs a pretty rig for a little while \xe2\x80\x94 her name is on every lip.
c.1850Peeping Tom (London) 9 32/3: The \xe2\x80\x98fine madams\xe2\x80\x99 were allowed to run their rigs without molestation.

\n In compounds\n

rig sale \n (n.)\n

a false sale, a mock auction.

1823\xe2\x80\x98Jon Bee\xe2\x80\x99 Dict. of the Turf, the Ring, the Chase, etc. 148: \xe2\x80\x98The rig;\xe2\x80\x99 in auction sales, the dealers agree not to bid against each other, buy low, and afterwards re-sell the same, by a mimick auction \xe2\x80\x94 called \xe2\x80\x98knock out.\xe2\x80\x99.
1851Chambers\xe2\x80\x99s Journal xv, 103: A pawnbroker contributes the linen, an exuberant quantity of which is generally one of the characteristics of the rig sale [F&H].
1909J. Ware Passing Eng. of the Victorian Era.

\n In phrases\n

run one\xe2\x80\x99s rig upon \n (v.)\n

to ridicule.

1764K. O\xe2\x80\x99Hara Midas II i: With his quips, and his quillets / And running his rigs.
1785, 1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue.
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
1823\xe2\x80\x98Jon Bee\xe2\x80\x99 Dict. of the Turf, the Ring, the Chase, etc. 148: He who has \xe2\x80\x98the rig run upon him,\xe2\x80\x99 has to undergo a great number of false imputations.
1840T. Haliburton Clockmaker III 37: It won\xe2\x80\x99t do to get cross when fellers are running of their rigs; it only makes them wus.
1848Joliet Signal (Joliet, IL) 18 Jan. 1/3: What do you mean by running your rigs on me jest because I\xe2\x80\x99m a stranger in these parts?
1863C. Reade Hard Cash II 16: It\xe2\x80\x99s only Jem\xe2\x80\x99s fun: he is allus running his rigs. [Ibid.] 94: Ye can\xe2\x80\x99t run your rigs on me.
run the rig on \n (v.)\n

1. to deceive, to trick.

1753Caledonian Mercury 15 May 1/2: \xe2\x80\x98You have run the rig upon me, Sir, and so I desire you will meet me to Morrow Morning in Pancreas Burying Ground\xe2\x80\x99.
1785\xe2\x80\x98Peter Pindar\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98Lousiad\xe2\x80\x99 Works (1794) I 277: For pot-boy and de pot-girl run der rig!
c.1790\xe2\x80\x98Peter Pindar\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98Pathetic Odes\xe2\x80\x99 Works (1794) III 431: Now on poor kingless France they run their rigs!
1798Sporting Mag. Aug. XII 282/1: Dear me! what fights I saw, when there! / So droll they run their riggs.
1819\xe2\x80\x98One of the Fancy\xe2\x80\x99 Tom Crib\xe2\x80\x99s Memorial to Congress 4: While snug and secure you may now run your rigs, / Without fear that old Boney will bother your gigs.
1821\xe2\x80\x936\xe2\x80\x98Bill Truck\xe2\x80\x99 Man o\xe2\x80\x99 War\xe2\x80\x99s Man (1843) 311: Don\xe2\x80\x99t go to be running the rig on us in that sly manner.
1837Thackeray Yellowplush Papers in Works III (1898) 264: Come, come, Mr. Deuceace, don\xe2\x80\x99t you be running your rigs upon me; I ain\xe2\x80\x99t the man to be bamboozl\xe2\x80\x99d.
1842Egan \xe2\x80\x98Miss Dolly Trull\xe2\x80\x99 in Farmer Musa Pedestris (1896) 143: She runs such precious cranky rigs / With pinching wedge and lockets.
1857 in Punch \xe2\x80\x98Dear Bill, This Stone-Jug\xe2\x80\x99 31 Jan. n.p.: In the dayrooms the cuffins we queers at our ease, / And at Darkmans we run the rig just as we please.
1862Morn. Post 18 Dec. 3/3: Oh, it\xe2\x80\x99s rare times for us prigs; in quod we runs our rigs. As merry as the grigs, on the best o\xe2\x80\x99 grub and snooze.

2. to mock, to ridicule.

1781Cumberland Pacquet 7 Aug. 4/1: Those who are still itching to run the Rig, may [...] cry, if they please, that the Subject is shocking.

\n In exclamations\n

go the rig, Maria!

an exhortation to commit oneself without any artifice or subtlety.

1824Pierce Egan\xe2\x80\x99s Life in London 26 Sept. 5/2: Much cautious manceuvring. (Cries of \xe2\x80\x98None of your science\xe2\x80\x94go the rig, Maria\xe2\x80\x99).
