Chuck n.

also Mr Chuck
[colloq. Chuck, the nickname derived from Charles; thus charles n.1 /Mr Charlie n. (1)]

1. (US black) a white man.

1965C. Brown Manchild in the Promised Land (1969) 347: That\xe2\x80\x99s the same thing Mr. Chuck\xe2\x80\x99s been doing all these years.
1972J.A. Williams Capt. Blackman 17: Knew it all the time [...] Chuck ain\xe2\x80\x99t shit. Talk about the Devil!
1972B. Rodgers Queens\xe2\x80\x99 Vernacular 63: chuck-lover (pej, black gay sl, fr black sl Charley = the white man) black man who prefers white sex partners.
1987(con. 1967) Bunch & Cole Reckoning for Kings (1989) 141: These chucks [...] still gonna think you a nigger.
1990(con. c.1970) G. Hasford Phantom Blooper 23: I no longer choose to participate in the mindset of morally disoriented bloodthirsty chucks.
2000Other Side of the Wall: Prisoner\xe2\x80\x99s Dict. July Chuck: A white prisoner or officer. (MI).

2. (US black) a man.

2010A. Steinberg Running the Books 82: A bitch like me can't be stuck on chuck, the boss is lost, for nada.
