monkey v.

also monkey about, monkey around, monkey with
[anthropomorphism]

1. (orig. US) to fool around, to tamper, to fiddle, usu. in a destructive clumsy manner, occas. used as a synon. for the expression \xe2\x80\x98to busy one\xe2\x80\x99s self\xe2\x80\x99 with anything, but it cannot be legitimately used of honest, useful work, except when such work is either badly done or is undertaken as a recreation rather than as a legitimate business.

1883N.Y. Times 26 Aug. 6/5: \xe2\x80\x98To monkey\xe2\x80\x99 is a neuter verb [...] Its primary meaning is to busy one\xe2\x80\x99s self in ways other than utilitarian. The amateur painter, or musician, \xe2\x80\x98monkeys\xe2\x80\x99 with art, and the political theorist who invents impracticable reforms may be said \xe2\x80\x98to monkey\xe2\x80\x99 with politics.
1884C.F. Lummis letter 1 Dec. in Byrkit Letters from the Southwest (1989) 122: Probably you think that I am monkeying around somewhere near the Azrizona line tonight.
1890Bulletin (Sydney) 16 Aug. 20/3: \xe2\x80\x98The court simply affirmed the decision of the lower court,\xe2\x80\x99 was the reply. \xe2\x80\x98And that is,\xe2\x80\x99 said Kemmler, \xe2\x80\x98that I\xe2\x80\x99ve got to be touched off by this electric machine. Well, the sooner it\xe2\x80\x99s over with now the better. I\xe2\x80\x99m tired of this monkeying for I guess the law\xe2\x80\x99s all right.\xe2\x80\x99.
1892E.J. Milliken \xe2\x80\x99Arry Ballads 6: Your monkeyings mar every pageant.
1896W.C. Gore Student Sl. in Cohen (1997) 11: monkey v. To fool; to fritter away one\xe2\x80\x99s time.
1903A. Binstead Pitcher in Paradise 123: The humiliating prospect of [...] explaining to an unfriendly alien that I had been monkeying with his money.
1905Marvel III:60 31: That\xe2\x80\x99s my \xe2\x80\x99and-cart you\xe2\x80\x99re monkeying around with!
1906S. Ford Shorty McCabe 8: Now as a general thing I don\xe2\x80\x99t monkey with the ponies. [Ibid.] 50: I wouldn\xe2\x80\x99t have monkeyed around after dark on that perpendicular landscape for twice the money.
1915Wodehouse Psmith Journalist (1993) 186: I can\xe2\x80\x99t go monkeying about with the paper that way.
1916A. Stringer Door of Dread 85: Yuh and your gorilla-gink \xe2\x80\x99ve done consider\xe2\x80\x99ble monkeyin\xe2\x80\x99 wit\xe2\x80\x99 me [...] and there\xe2\x80\x99s been doin\xe2\x80\x99s in this dump that\xe2\x80\x99ll call for consider\xe2\x80\x99ble ventilatin\xe2\x80\x99.
1917F. Packard Adventures of Jimmie Dale (1918) I ii: I\xe2\x80\x99ll show you how it\xe2\x80\x99s stuck on, if you monkey around here!
1921P. & T. Casey Gay-cat 44: A yegger! [...] A strong-armer! Now I know, I don\xe2\x80\x99t monkey with Mr. Whitey-scar.
1922M.E. Smith Adventures of a Boomer Op. 11: It looked to me that to monkey around getting him riled up, would be too much like flirting with Kingdom Come.
1923Wodehouse Leave it to Psmith (1993) 516: I\xe2\x80\x99ve got no time to waste monkeying about with safes.
1932(con. WWI) L. Mann Flesh in Armour 263: \xe2\x80\x98War\xe2\x80\x99s too big a thing [...] to monkey about finding out that this lord general\xe2\x80\x99s a dud\xe2\x80\x99.
1933J. Spenser Limey 35: He\xe2\x80\x99s been monkeyin\xe2\x80\x99 around with another guy\xe2\x80\x99s girl.
1935A. Christie Three Act Tragedy (1964) 143: Doctors are interfering devils [...] They monkey about with fellows\xe2\x80\x99 lives.
1938D. Runyon \xe2\x80\x98Dancing Dan\xe2\x80\x99s Christmas\xe2\x80\x99 in Runyon on Broadway (1954) 257: He is not a guy to monkey with.
1947F. Brown Fabulous Clipjoint (1949) 109: We\xe2\x80\x99ve monkeyed around with him too long.
1947N. Marsh Final Curtain (1958) 182: Supposing Miss Orrincourt did monkey with the thermos.
1959E. De Roo Go, Man, Go! 81: At the garage [... the Corvette was all they had in there. Three of them monkeyed with it.
1960P. Rabe Murder Me for Nickels (2004) 27: Nobody fixes the gadgets except us, nobody monkeys with the wires.
1966P. Willmott Adolescent Boys of East London (1969) 139: You go in and you mess about and you start getting told off and before long you\xe2\x80\x99re monkeying about and playing up.
1987B. Chatwin Songlines 159: Make sure the kids don\xe2\x80\x99t monkey around with the Land Cruiser.
2000F. Kellerman Stalker (2001) 174: The car had been monkeyed with prior to the kidnapping.
2000Observer Rev. 19 Mar. 6: We don\xe2\x80\x99t like the way he is proposing to monkey about with the Brunswick Centre.
2006News Jrnl (Mansfield, OH) 17 Dec. 25/1: When a husband would rather monkey around with his monkey than monkey around with his wife, you know the marriage is in serious trouble.
2014E. Pruitt \xe2\x80\x98Houston\xe2\x80\x99 in ThugLit Mar. [ebook] \xe2\x80\x98Never monkey with the supply\xe2\x80\x99.
2020D. Winslow \xe2\x80\x98The San Diego Zoo\xe2\x80\x99 in Broken 129: He loses track of the times he\xe2\x80\x99s told not to “monkey around on his shift.

2. (US) to cheat, to defraud.

1889Nat. Police Gaz. (NY) 23 Nov. 6/2: The next story on the list is [...] how a Mrs. Craig monkeyed old man Craig out of $1,000.

\n In phrases\n

monkey with the band wagon \n (v.)\n

(US campus) to interfere, to meddle.

1896W.C. Gore Student Sl. in Cohen (1997) 11: monkey with the band wagon. To interfere, to meddle with. Synonymous with \xe2\x80\x98to monkey with the buzz-saw\xe2\x80\x99.
monkey with a/the buzz saw \n (v.)\n

to interfere foolishly.

1886Nat. Police Gaz. (NY) 10 Apr. 2/1: [headline] Monkeying with the Buzz Saw.
1893Harper\xe2\x80\x99s Mag. 87 July 307/2: We discover immediately the daringly condensed metaphor in the sign, \xe2\x80\x98Don\xe2\x80\x99t monkey with the buzz-saw\xe2\x80\x99; the picturesqueness of the word buzz-saw and its fitness for service are visible at a glance.
1909Pacific Printer 223: Some publisher of experience should have warned Mr. Waltz not to monkey with the buzz saw of journalism.
1914I.S. Cobb Europe Rev. 378: The motto in both instances being in effect: \xe2\x80\x98Don\xe2\x80\x99t monkey with the buzz saw.\xe2\x80\x99.
1921Dos Passos Three Soldiers 115: I tell you, fellers, it don\xe2\x80\x99t do to monkey with the buzz-saw in this army.
1929(con. WW1) Hall & Niles One Man\xe2\x80\x99s War 100: It was a great lesson to me\xe2\x80\x94never monkey with a buzz-saw in the air. And a hand grenade is the nearest thing to a buzz-saw I can think of.
1932(con. 1917\xe2\x80\x9319) Dos Passos Nineteen Nineteen in USA (1966) 652: That comes of monkeying with the buzzsaw.
1939C. Beals Amer. Earth: The Biography of a Nation 293: [chapter heading] XV DON\xe2\x80\x99T MONKEY WITH THE BUZZ-SAW.
1960A.B. Miller Thaddeus Stevens 264: That would teach Sumner not to monkey with the buzz-saw!
