fly n.1

[SE fly, a fast carriage, a stage-coach]

(UK Und.) a wagon.

1703Hell Upon Earth 5: Fly, a Waggon, i.e. Country Cart.
1708J. Hall Memoirs (1714) 12: Fly, a Waggon, i.e. a Countrey Cart.
1788, 1796Grose Classical Dict. of the Vulgar Tongue (2nd, 3rd edn).
1811Lex. Balatronicum.
c.1821C. Sloman \xe2\x80\x98The Good Old Times\xe2\x80\x99 in Matthews Cockney Past and Present 85: Vot they vonce call\xe2\x80\x99d a vaggon is now called a fly.

\n In phrases\n

on the fly [SE fly, to move quickly]

1. (US) in a hurry, on the move, spontaneously.

1842Edinburgh Rev. July 484: Cadgers on the fly are those who beg as they pass along the tober (road). Cadging on the fly is a profitable occupation in the vicinity of bathing-places and large towns.
1868 \xe2\x80\x98Six Years in the Prisons of England\xe2\x80\x99 in Temple Bar Mag. Nov. 537: Picking pockets [...] is more difficult on the \xe2\x80\x98fly\xe2\x80\x99.
1874 \xe2\x80\x98English Sl.\xe2\x80\x99 in Eve. Telegram (N.Y.) 9 Dec. 1/5: Let us present a few specimens:\xe2\x80\x93 [...] \xe2\x80\x98On the fly.\xe2\x80\x99.
c.1880 \xe2\x80\x98US Army Sl. 1870s\xe2\x80\x931880s\xe2\x80\x99 [compiled by R. Bunting, San Diego CA, 2001] On the fly In a hurry; in motion.
1935J. Conroy World to Win 62: I nails a grab iron on the fly.
1940B. Appel People Talk (1972) 272: You eat your dinner on the fly so you can finish drillin\xe2\x80\x99 by four o\xe2\x80\x99clock.
1954Kerouac letter 2 Jan. in Charters I (1995) 427: And my God if Burroughs goes there I\xe2\x80\x99m sure to come on the fly.
1969M. Puzo Godfather 145: I\xe2\x80\x99ll spot you and put on my headlights and catch you on the fly.
1977L. Heinemann Close Quarters (1987) 47: I snatch up the strap of the pig on the fly.
1989J. Morton Lowspeak.
1993Eble Campus Sl. Apr. 4: on the fly \xe2\x80\x93 hurriedly. \xe2\x80\x98I ate a burger on the fly.\xe2\x80\x99.
1999Guardian Rev. 15 Oct. 21: His on-the-fly imagination.
2012L. Berney Whiplash River [ebook] Gina\xe2\x80\x99s abiliy to lie on the fly was scary.
2013T. Robinson Hard Bounce [ebook] \xe2\x80\x98We\xe2\x80\x99ll do this on the fly. let\xe2\x80\x99s see where she goes first\xe2\x80\x99.

2. (US tramp) while moving, usu. describing the boarding of a (freight) train; often as catch it on the fly.

1907J. London Road 159: Wait till you hit the Pennsylvania, four tracks, no water tanks, take water on the fly, that\xe2\x80\x99s goin\xe2\x80\x99 some.
1917\xe2\x80\x98A-No. 1\xe2\x80\x99 From Coast to Coast with Jack London 24: See our train taking water on the fly!
1929E. Dahlberg Bottom Dogs 178: He caught a streetcar on the fly which happened to be going down to the railroad station.
1930C.R. Shaw Jack-Roller 128: I waited at the depot and caught the Omaha Limited \xe2\x80\x98on the fly\xe2\x80\x99.
c.1935J. Wright \xe2\x80\x98Battler\xe2\x80\x99s Ballad\xe2\x80\x99 in Seal (1999) 97: With your swag held at the ready, your nerves are not so steady / For you know you\xe2\x80\x99ve got to take her on the fly.
1966B. Jackson Get Your Ass in the Water (1974) 71: I\xe2\x80\x99ve toted a pack down a B and O track / hopped redball freights on the fly.
1990(con. 1930s\xe2\x80\x9360s) H. Huncke Guilty of Everything (1998) 238: We waited \xe2\x80\x99til they linked up the various cars and gave the highball, and then we caught it on the fly.

3. cunningly, clandestinely, in secret.

1929C. McKay Banjo 317: They were all going \xe2\x80\x98on the fly\xe2\x80\x99 and none of them was thinking of staying with the boat after the trip, but rather of getting to Cuba, Canada, and the United States.
1930 in E. Cray Erotic Muse (1992) 190: So we pulled out our guns and we got him on the fly, / Crawled in the weeds, and I guess he\xe2\x80\x99s going to die.
1949N. Algren Man with the Golden Arm 17: He had snatched snipes, on the fly.
1950\xe2\x80\x98Josephine Tey\xe2\x80\x99 To Love and Be Wise 154: \xe2\x80\x98You got the car out on the fly?\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98No,\xe2\x80\x99 Grant said, and asked why she should think that. \xe2\x80\x98Oh [...] Thought maybe you were out for the day on your little own\xe2\x80\x99 .
1993I. Rankin Black Book (2000) 133: He knew they were decorators because they\xe2\x80\x99d asked him if he needed any work doing. \xe2\x80\x98On the fly, like. Cheaper that way.\xe2\x80\x99.

4. by mistake.

1975E. Torres Carlito\xe2\x80\x99s Way 126: I\xe2\x80\x99m here on the fly, caught up in the web.

5. (US black) living in an expensive, fashionable, self-indulgent manner.

1994G. Smitherman Black Talk.
